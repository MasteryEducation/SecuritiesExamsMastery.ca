## 2.15 Alternative Trading Systems

In the evolving landscape of financial markets, Alternative Trading Systems (ATS) have emerged as pivotal platforms that complement traditional exchanges. This section delves into the intricacies of ATS, their functioning, and their impact on the Canadian financial markets.

### Defining Alternative Trading Systems (ATS)

Alternative Trading Systems (ATS) are electronic platforms that facilitate the trading of securities outside of traditional stock exchanges. Unlike conventional exchanges, ATS are not bound by the same regulatory framework, allowing for more flexibility in their operations. They serve as additional venues for trading, providing investors with more options and potentially better pricing.

#### Differences from Traditional Exchanges and Dealer Markets

Traditional exchanges, such as the Toronto Stock Exchange (TSX), operate as centralized marketplaces where securities are listed and traded. They are highly regulated and provide transparency and liquidity. In contrast, ATS operate with less stringent regulations, offering a more private trading environment. Dealer markets, on the other hand, involve transactions conducted over-the-counter (OTC) where dealers facilitate trades. ATS differ by providing a platform for direct buyer-seller interaction without the need for a dealer intermediary.

#### Role of ATS in Securities Trading

ATS play a crucial role in enhancing market efficiency by offering alternative venues for trading. They increase competition among trading platforms, which can lead to better pricing and reduced transaction costs for investors. By providing additional liquidity, ATS contribute to the overall robustness of the financial markets.

#### Competition with Recognized Exchanges

ATS compete with traditional exchanges by offering lower fees, faster execution times, and innovative trading solutions. This competition can drive exchanges to improve their services and reduce costs, ultimately benefiting investors. However, ATS also face competition among themselves, striving to attract more participants by offering unique features and superior technology.

### Understanding the Functioning of ATS

The core functionality of ATS lies in their ability to automate the matching and execution of trades. This automation is achieved through sophisticated technology and platforms that enable electronic trading.

#### Automated Matching and Execution

ATS utilize algorithms to match buy and sell orders based on predefined criteria such as price and volume. This process is known as automated trade matching, which enhances the speed and efficiency of transactions. By eliminating manual intervention, ATS reduce the likelihood of errors and delays.

#### Technology and Platforms

The technology underpinning ATS includes advanced software and hardware systems capable of handling large volumes of transactions. These platforms are designed to provide real-time data, analytics, and execution capabilities. They often integrate with other financial systems to ensure seamless trading experiences.

#### Regulatory Requirements

Operating an ATS in Canada requires adherence to specific regulatory requirements. ATS must register with the Investment Industry Regulatory Organization of Canada (IIROC) and comply with its rules. They are also subject to oversight by provincial securities regulators. Membership in self-regulatory organizations (SROs) is mandatory to ensure compliance with industry standards and protect investor interests.

### Exploring Equity Electronic Trading Systems

Equity electronic trading systems are a subset of ATS focused on the trading of equity securities. They play a significant role in modern equity markets by facilitating efficient and transparent transactions.

#### Role in Modern Equity Markets

Equity ATS provide a platform for trading stocks and other equity instruments outside traditional exchanges. They offer features such as anonymity, which can be attractive to institutional investors looking to execute large trades without impacting market prices.

#### Benefits of Automated Trade Matching

Automated trade matching in equity ATS leads to faster execution and reduced transaction costs. It also enhances market liquidity by enabling continuous trading and providing access to a broader pool of buyers and sellers.

#### Impact on Market Liquidity and Trading Efficiency

By offering additional venues for trading, equity ATS contribute to increased market liquidity. They allow for more efficient price discovery and can lead to tighter bid-ask spreads. This efficiency benefits both investors and issuers by providing more accurate pricing and reducing the cost of capital.

#### Examples of Equity ATS in Canada

In Canada, several equity ATS operate alongside traditional exchanges. Examples include Alpha Trading Systems and MATCHNow. These platforms offer unique features such as dark pools, where trades are executed without pre-trade transparency, allowing for large block trades without market impact.

### Exploring Fixed-Income Electronic Trading Systems

Fixed-income electronic trading systems are designed for the trading of bonds and money market instruments. They provide a platform for efficient and transparent fixed-income transactions.

#### Role in Bond and Money Market Trading

These systems facilitate the trading of fixed-income securities by providing electronic platforms for buyers and sellers to interact. They enhance market transparency and provide access to a wide range of fixed-income products.

#### Features and Services

Fixed-income trading systems offer features such as real-time pricing, analytics, and execution capabilities. They provide services like price discovery, trade execution, and post-trade processing. Notable systems in Canada include CanDeal, CBID, MarketAxess, and CanPX.

#### Enhancing Trading Efficiency and Access

By automating the trading process, fixed-income systems improve efficiency and reduce transaction costs. They provide access to a broader range of securities and enable investors to execute trades quickly and accurately.

#### Integration within the Broader Market Infrastructure

These systems are integrated into the broader fixed-income market infrastructure, providing connectivity with clearing and settlement systems. This integration ensures that trades are processed smoothly and efficiently, reducing operational risks.

### Assessing the Impact of ATS on Financial Markets

ATS have a profound impact on financial markets by driving innovation and competition. They offer numerous benefits but also face challenges in terms of regulation and market acceptance.

#### Contribution to Market Innovation and Competition

ATS contribute to market innovation by introducing new trading technologies and practices. They foster competition among trading venues, leading to better services and lower costs for investors.

#### Benefits of Increased Trading Options

The availability of multiple trading venues provides investors with more options and flexibility. It can lead to better pricing and increased liquidity, benefiting both investors and issuers.

#### Challenges in Regulation and Market Acceptance

Despite their benefits, ATS face challenges related to regulation and market acceptance. They must navigate complex regulatory environments and gain the trust of market participants. Ensuring compliance with regulatory standards is crucial for their success.

#### Future Trends and Growth Areas

The future of ATS in Canadian financial markets looks promising, with potential growth areas including the expansion of electronic trading in fixed-income markets and the development of new trading technologies. As technology continues to evolve, ATS are likely to play an increasingly important role in the financial ecosystem.

### Glossary

- **Alternative Trading System (ATS):** An electronic trading system that matches buyers and sellers for securities outside of traditional stock exchanges.
- **Exchange-Traded Funds (ETFs):** Funds traded on stock exchanges that hold assets such as stocks, commodities, or bonds.
- **Investment Industry Regulatory Organization of Canada (IIROC):** A self-regulatory organization overseeing all investment dealers and trading activity on debt and equity marketplaces in Canada.
- **Automated Trade Matching:** The process by which buy and sell orders are matched and executed automatically by trading systems.
