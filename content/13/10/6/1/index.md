## 10.6.1 Rights

In the realm of Canadian securities, rights are a pivotal financial instrument that offer existing shareholders the opportunity to purchase additional shares of a company at a predetermined price, often below the current market value. This section delves into the mechanics of rights, including the subscription price, the process of exercising rights, and the calculation of intrinsic value. We will also provide a practical example to illustrate these concepts in action.

### Subscription Price and Strike Price

The **subscription price** is a key feature of rights, representing the price at which existing shareholders can purchase additional shares. This price is typically set below the current market price of the shares, providing an incentive for shareholders to exercise their rights. By offering shares at a discount, companies can raise capital more effectively, as shareholders are more likely to participate in the rights offering.

For example, if a company's shares are trading at $50 on the open market, a rights offering might allow shareholders to purchase additional shares at a subscription price of $40. This discount is designed to make the rights offering attractive, encouraging shareholders to invest further in the company.

### Exercise of Rights

Exercising rights involves a specific process and set of conditions. Shareholders must decide whether to exercise their rights within a given timeframe, known as the **rights offering period**. During this period, shareholders can choose to:

1. **Exercise the Rights:** Purchase additional shares at the subscription price.
2. **Sell the Rights:** Rights can often be traded on the open market, allowing shareholders to sell their rights to others.
3. **Let the Rights Expire:** If shareholders do not wish to purchase additional shares or sell their rights, they can let the rights expire, although this means forfeiting any potential value.

The decision to exercise rights depends on several factors, including the current market price of the shares, the subscription price, and the shareholder's investment strategy.

### Intrinsic Value Calculation

The intrinsic value of a right is a crucial concept, reflecting the potential profit from exercising the right. This value can be calculated differently depending on whether the shares are in the **ex-rights period** or the **cum rights period**.

#### Ex-Rights Period

During the ex-rights period, the rights have been separated from the underlying shares. The intrinsic value of a right in this period can be calculated using the formula:

{{< katex >}} \text{Intrinsic Value (Ex-Rights)} = \text{Market Price of Share} - \text{Subscription Price} {{< /katex >}}

#### Cum Rights Period

In the cum rights period, the rights are still attached to the shares. The intrinsic value calculation is slightly more complex:

{{< katex >}} \text{Intrinsic Value (Cum Rights)} = \left( \frac{\text{Market Price of Share} - \text{Subscription Price}}{\text{Number of Rights Required to Purchase One Share} + 1} \right) {{< /katex >}}

### Example of Rights

Let's walk through an example to illustrate the intrinsic value calculation of rights.

**Scenario:**

- Market Price of Share: $50
- Subscription Price: $40
- Number of Rights Required to Purchase One Share: 4

**Ex-Rights Period Calculation:**

{{< katex >}} \text{Intrinsic Value (Ex-Rights)} = 50 - 40 = \$10 {{< /katex >}}

**Cum Rights Period Calculation:**

{{< katex >}} \text{Intrinsic Value (Cum Rights)} = \left( \frac{50 - 40}{4 + 1} \right) = \left( \frac{10}{5} \right) = \$2 {{< /katex >}}

In this example, during the ex-rights period, each right has an intrinsic value of $10, while during the cum rights period, the intrinsic value is $2 per right.

### Glossary

- **Subscription Price:** The price at which a right allows the purchase of new shares.
- **Ex-Rights Period:** The period after which rights are separated from the underlying shares.

### Best Practices and Considerations

When dealing with rights, investors should consider the following best practices:

- **Evaluate Market Conditions:** Assess the current market price relative to the subscription price to determine the potential value of exercising rights.
- **Understand the Timeframe:** Be aware of the rights offering period and make timely decisions to avoid letting rights expire without action.
- **Consider Tax Implications:** Understand the tax consequences of exercising or selling rights, as these can impact overall investment returns.

### Conclusion

Rights are a valuable tool for both companies seeking to raise capital and investors looking to increase their holdings at a discount. By understanding the subscription price, exercise process, and intrinsic value calculations, investors can make informed decisions that align with their financial goals. As with any investment decision, careful analysis and consideration of market conditions are essential.
