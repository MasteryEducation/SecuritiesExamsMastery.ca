## 20.9.1 Mutual Funds of ETFs

Mutual funds of ETFs represent a unique and innovative approach to investment, combining the benefits of mutual funds with the flexibility and cost-effectiveness of exchange-traded funds (ETFs). This section delves into the structure, benefits, investment strategies, and fee structures of mutual funds of ETFs, providing a comprehensive understanding of their role in modern investment portfolios.

### Structure and Composition

Mutual funds of ETFs are investment vehicles designed to hold a diversified portfolio of multiple ETFs. This structure allows investors to gain broad market exposure and strategic diversification through a single investment product. By investing in a mutual fund of ETFs, investors can access a wide array of asset classes, sectors, and geographic regions without the need to purchase individual ETFs separately.

The composition of these funds typically includes a mix of ETFs that track various indices, sectors, or themes. This diversified approach helps mitigate risk by spreading investments across different market segments. The portfolio manager plays a crucial role in selecting and managing the ETF holdings to align with the fund's investment objectives.

### Benefits

#### Automatic Rebalancing

One of the key advantages of mutual funds of ETFs is automatic rebalancing. As market conditions change, the value of different ETFs within the fund may fluctuate, causing the asset allocation to drift from its target. Mutual funds of ETFs automatically adjust the portfolio to maintain the desired asset distribution, ensuring that the investment strategy remains aligned with the investor's goals.

#### Access to PACs and SWPs

Mutual funds of ETFs offer the convenience of pre-authorized contributions (PACs) and systematic withdrawal plans (SWPs), similar to traditional mutual funds. PACs allow investors to make regular, automatic contributions to their investment accounts, facilitating disciplined saving and investment. SWPs provide a mechanism for investors to receive regular, automatic withdrawals, which can be particularly beneficial for retirees seeking a steady income stream.

#### Lower MERs

Mutual funds of ETFs generally have lower management expense ratios (MERs) compared to traditional mutual funds. This cost efficiency is primarily due to the passive management of the underlying ETFs, which typically have lower fees than actively managed mutual funds. The reduced MERs contribute to higher net returns for investors over the long term.

#### Diversification without High Minimum Investments

Investing directly in multiple ETFs can require significant capital commitments, which may not be feasible for all investors. Mutual funds of ETFs provide diversified exposure without the need for large initial investments. This accessibility makes them an attractive option for investors seeking diversification without the burden of high minimum investment requirements.

### Investment Strategies

Mutual funds of ETFs can implement a variety of investment strategies by selecting different ETFs. These strategies can range from broad market index tracking to more specialized sector or thematic investments. For example, a mutual fund of ETFs might include ETFs that focus on technology, healthcare, or sustainable investing, depending on the fund's objectives.

The portfolio manager's expertise is crucial in selecting the appropriate ETFs to achieve the desired investment outcomes. By carefully analyzing market trends and economic indicators, the manager can adjust the ETF holdings to optimize performance and manage risk.

### Fee Structure

The fee structure of mutual funds of ETFs is typically more favorable compared to traditional mutual funds. The aggregation of ETFs within the fund allows for lower management fees, contributing to overall cost efficiency. This fee advantage, combined with the diversified holdings, makes mutual funds of ETFs an appealing choice for cost-conscious investors.

### Examples of Mutual Funds of ETFs

Several mutual funds in Canada offer portfolios of ETFs, each with distinct investment objectives and strategies. For instance, a mutual fund of ETFs might aim to provide global equity exposure by holding a mix of international and domestic ETFs. Another fund might focus on income generation by including ETFs that invest in dividend-paying stocks or fixed-income securities.

Analyzing the performance of these funds reveals their effectiveness in managing diversified ETF portfolios. By leveraging the strengths of various ETFs, these funds can achieve competitive returns while maintaining a balanced risk profile.

### Glossary

- **Fund of ETFs:** An investment fund that holds a diversified portfolio of multiple ETFs.
- **Pre-Authorized Contributions (PACs):** Regular, automatic contributions made to a mutual fund account.
- **Systematic Withdrawal Plans (SWPs):** Regular, automatic withdrawals made from a mutual fund account.
- **Management Expense Ratio (MER):** The total annual fees expressed as a percentage of the fund’s average net assets, covering management and operational costs.

### Conclusion

Mutual funds of ETFs offer a compelling investment option for those seeking diversification, cost efficiency, and convenience. By combining the benefits of mutual funds and ETFs, these investment vehicles provide broad market exposure with the added advantages of automatic rebalancing and flexible contribution and withdrawal options. As the investment landscape continues to evolve, mutual funds of ETFs are likely to play an increasingly important role in portfolio management.
