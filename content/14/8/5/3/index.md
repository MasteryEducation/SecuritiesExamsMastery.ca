## 20.5.3 Fund of Hedge Funds

In the realm of alternative investments, Fund of Hedge Funds (FoHF) represents a sophisticated vehicle designed to offer investors access to a diversified portfolio of hedge funds. This section delves into the structure, types, benefits, costs, and risks associated with FoHFs, providing a comprehensive understanding for finance professionals and students.

### Definition and Structure

A Fund of Hedge Funds (FoHF) is an investment vehicle that pools capital from multiple investors to allocate it across a selection of hedge funds. This structure allows investors to gain exposure to a variety of hedge fund strategies, potentially enhancing returns while mitigating risks through diversification. The FoHF manager is responsible for selecting and managing the portfolio of hedge funds, conducting due diligence, and monitoring performance.

### Types of Fund of Hedge Funds

FoHFs can be categorized into two primary types based on their investment strategies:

#### Single-Strategy FoHF

Single-Strategy FoHFs focus on hedge funds that employ a similar investment strategy. For instance, a FoHF might invest exclusively in hedge funds that specialize in long/short equity strategies. This approach allows investors to capitalize on specific market opportunities while benefiting from the expertise of multiple managers within the same strategy.

#### Multi-Strategy FoHF

Multi-Strategy FoHFs invest in hedge funds employing a diverse range of strategies, such as global macro, event-driven, and relative value. This diversification provides broader exposure to different market conditions and reduces the risk associated with any single strategy. Multi-Strategy FoHFs aim to deliver more stable returns by balancing the performance of various strategies.

### Benefits of Fund of Hedge Funds

FoHFs offer several advantages that make them appealing to investors seeking alternative investment opportunities:

#### Diversification

By spreading investments across multiple hedge funds, FoHFs reduce the risk associated with individual fund performance. This diversification can enhance the potential for higher returns while mitigating volatility.

#### Due Diligence

Professional managers of FoHFs conduct thorough due diligence on the underlying hedge funds, assessing their strategies, performance, and risk management practices. This rigorous evaluation helps ensure that only high-quality hedge funds are included in the portfolio.

#### Access to Multiple Strategies

FoHFs provide investors with access to a wide range of hedge fund strategies that might be inaccessible individually due to high minimum investment requirements or limited availability. This access allows investors to benefit from the expertise of top hedge fund managers across various strategies.

### Costs and Fees

Investing in FoHFs involves an additional layer of fees compared to direct hedge fund investments. These fees typically include:

- **Management Fees:** Charged by the FoHF manager for managing the portfolio of hedge funds.
- **Performance-Based Incentive Fees:** Additional fees based on the performance of the FoHF, on top of those charged by the underlying hedge funds.

These fees can significantly impact overall returns, making it essential for investors to carefully evaluate the cost structure of a FoHF.

### Risks and Disadvantages

While FoHFs offer several benefits, they also come with inherent risks and disadvantages:

#### Higher Fees

The multiple layers of fees associated with FoHFs can erode overall returns, making it crucial for investors to weigh the cost against the potential benefits.

#### Potential Dilution of Returns

Over-diversification within a FoHF can lead to alpha dilution, where the unique value-added by individual hedge funds is diminished. This can result in more muted performance compared to investing directly in a select few hedge funds.

#### Dependence on FoHF Manager

The success of a FoHF largely depends on the expertise and decision-making of the FoHF manager. Poor selection or management of underlying hedge funds can adversely affect the FoHF's performance.

### Examples and Case Studies

To illustrate the practical application of FoHFs, consider the following examples:

#### Example 1: Canadian Pension Fund

A Canadian pension fund seeking to diversify its portfolio might invest in a Multi-Strategy FoHF. By doing so, the pension fund gains exposure to various hedge fund strategies, such as global macro and event-driven, without the need to directly manage individual hedge fund investments.

#### Example 2: RBC's Investment Approach

RBC, one of Canada's largest banks, may offer a Single-Strategy FoHF focused on long/short equity hedge funds. This FoHF allows RBC's clients to benefit from the expertise of multiple hedge fund managers specializing in equity markets, providing a targeted investment approach.

### Glossary

- **Fund of Hedge Funds (FoHF):** An investment fund that allocates capital to a portfolio of multiple hedge funds.
- **Alpha Dilution:** The reduction of alpha gain due to over-diversification.
- **Diversification:** The practice of spreading investments across various assets to reduce risk.

### Conclusion

Fund of Hedge Funds offer a unique opportunity for investors to access a diversified portfolio of hedge fund strategies. While they provide benefits such as diversification and professional due diligence, investors must carefully consider the costs and potential risks associated with these investment vehicles. By understanding the structure and dynamics of FoHFs, investors can make informed decisions that align with their financial goals.
