## 20.5.2 Alternative Mutual Funds (Liquid Alts)

Alternative mutual funds, commonly referred to as "liquid alts," represent a significant evolution in the investment landscape, particularly within the Canadian market. These funds integrate alternative investment strategies within a mutual fund structure, offering retail investors access to sophisticated strategies traditionally reserved for institutional investors and high-net-worth individuals. In this section, we will delve into the definition, structure, regulatory framework, investment strategies, and other critical aspects of liquid alts.

### Definition and Structure

Liquid alts are mutual funds that employ alternative investment strategies, such as hedge fund-like tactics, within the regulatory framework of mutual funds. These strategies may include the use of derivatives, leverage, and short selling, which are typically associated with hedge funds. However, unlike traditional hedge funds, liquid alts offer the liquidity and transparency of mutual funds, allowing investors to trade and redeem shares on a daily basis.

The structure of liquid alts is designed to provide investors with the benefits of alternative strategies while maintaining the regulatory protections and accessibility of mutual funds. This hybrid approach allows for greater diversification and the potential for enhanced returns, making liquid alts an attractive option for investors seeking to expand their portfolios beyond traditional asset classes.

### Regulatory Framework

The creation of liquid alts in Canada was facilitated by the modernization of National Instrument 81-102 (NI 81-102), which governs mutual funds. This regulatory update allowed for the inclusion of alternative strategies within mutual funds, subject to specific requirements and restrictions.

#### Key Regulatory Requirements and Restrictions:

- **Derivatives and Leverage:** Liquid alts are permitted to use derivatives and leverage, but within prescribed limits to mitigate risk. For example, the total exposure from derivatives and leverage must not exceed 300% of the fund's net asset value.
- **Short Selling:** These funds can engage in short selling, but the short positions are typically limited to 50% of the fund's net asset value.
- **Liquidity and Transparency:** Liquid alts must adhere to the same liquidity and transparency standards as conventional mutual funds, ensuring daily valuation and redemption capabilities.

These regulatory measures are designed to protect investors while allowing fund managers the flexibility to implement alternative strategies.

### Investment Strategies

Liquid alts employ a variety of alternative investment strategies to achieve their objectives. Some of the most common strategies include:

- **Relative Value Strategy:** This strategy seeks to exploit pricing discrepancies between related securities. For example, a liquid alt might simultaneously buy and sell different securities that are expected to converge in price, profiting from the relative price movement.
  
- **Event-Driven Strategy:** This approach focuses on corporate events such as mergers, acquisitions, and restructurings. Fund managers aim to capitalize on the price movements resulting from these events, often using derivatives to hedge risks.

- **Directional Strategy:** Involves taking long or short positions based on the anticipated direction of market movements. This strategy may involve significant use of leverage and derivatives to amplify returns.

The use of these strategies within the constraints of mutual fund regulations allows liquid alts to offer a unique risk-return profile compared to traditional mutual funds.

### Liquidity and Transparency

One of the defining features of liquid alts is their mutual fund-like liquidity. Investors can buy and sell shares on a daily basis, providing a level of flexibility not typically available with traditional hedge funds. This daily liquidity is a significant advantage for investors who require access to their capital.

In terms of transparency, liquid alts are required to provide regular disclosures similar to those of conventional mutual funds. This includes detailed information on holdings, performance, and fees, offering investors greater insight into the fund's operations compared to the opaque nature of many hedge funds.

### Fee Structure

The fee structure of liquid alts is generally more favorable than that of hedge funds. While they may incorporate both management and performance fees, these fees are typically lower than those charged by hedge funds. This makes liquid alts a cost-effective option for investors seeking exposure to alternative strategies without the high fees associated with hedge funds.

### Investor Access

Liquid alts democratize access to alternative investment strategies, allowing retail investors to participate in strategies that were once the domain of accredited investors. They offer lower minimum investment requirements compared to hedge funds, making them accessible to a broader range of investors.

### Comparison with Conventional Mutual Funds

When compared to conventional mutual funds, liquid alts offer greater strategy flexibility and the potential for higher returns. However, they also come with increased risk due to the use of leverage and derivatives. Investors should carefully consider their risk tolerance and investment objectives when evaluating liquid alts.

| Feature                  | Liquid Alts                      | Conventional Mutual Funds       |
|--------------------------|----------------------------------|---------------------------------|
| Strategy Flexibility     | High (alternative strategies)    | Low (traditional strategies)    |
| Risk                     | Higher (due to leverage/derivatives) | Lower                           |
| Return Potential         | Potentially higher               | Moderate                        |
| Liquidity                | Daily                            | Daily                           |
| Transparency             | High                             | High                            |
| Fee Structure            | Moderate (lower than hedge funds) | Low                             |

### Glossary

- **Liquid Alts:** Alternative mutual funds that offer alternative investment strategies with mutual fund-like liquidity and transparency.
- **Derivatives:** Financial instruments whose value derives from the value of an underlying asset.
- **Relative Value Strategy:** A strategy aiming to profit from pricing discrepancies between related securities.
- **Event-Driven Strategy:** A strategy focusing on corporate actions such as mergers, acquisitions, and restructurings.

### Conclusion

Alternative mutual funds, or liquid alts, represent a compelling option for investors seeking to diversify their portfolios with alternative strategies while maintaining the liquidity and transparency of mutual funds. By understanding the regulatory framework, investment strategies, and fee structures, investors can make informed decisions about incorporating liquid alts into their investment portfolios.
