## Chapter 20: Alternative Investments: Benefits, Risks, and Structure

### 20.8 Identify the Investment Strategies Involving ETFs

Exchange-Traded Funds (ETFs) have revolutionized the investment landscape, offering a versatile tool for implementing a variety of investment strategies. This section explores the diverse strategies that leverage ETFs, providing insights into their application and benefits within the Canadian financial context.

### Introduction to ETF-Based Strategies

ETFs are popular among investors for their liquidity, diversification, and cost-effectiveness. They can be used in several strategic ways, including:

- **Core and Satellite Strategy**
- **Rebalancing**
- **Tactical Asset Allocation**
- **Cash Management**
- **Exposure to Hard-to-Access Markets**
- **Tax Loss Harvesting**

Each strategy offers unique advantages and can be tailored to meet specific investment goals.

### Core and Satellite Strategy

The core and satellite strategy is a popular investment approach that combines stability with targeted growth. In this strategy:

- **Core Holdings**: These consist of broad market ETFs that provide a stable foundation for the portfolio. Examples include ETFs tracking the S&P/TSX Composite Index, which offer broad exposure to the Canadian market.
- **Satellite Holdings**: These are specialized or sector-specific ETFs that aim to enhance returns or target specific investment themes. For instance, an investor might include a technology-focused ETF to capitalize on growth in that sector.

**Benefits**: This strategy balances the stability of core holdings with the potential for higher returns from satellite investments. It allows investors to maintain a diversified portfolio while pursuing specific growth opportunities.

### Rebalancing

Rebalancing is the process of realigning the proportions of assets in a portfolio to maintain a desired asset allocation. ETFs facilitate efficient rebalancing due to their liquidity and tradability.

**Rebalancing Strategies**:
- **Periodic Rebalancing**: Adjusting the portfolio at regular intervals, such as quarterly or annually.
- **Threshold-Based Rebalancing**: Rebalancing when asset allocations deviate from set thresholds.
- **Automatic Rebalancing**: Using automated tools to maintain target allocations.

**Example**: A Canadian investor with a 60/40 equity-to-bond allocation might use ETFs to rebalance their portfolio when market movements cause the allocation to shift significantly.

### Tactical Asset Allocation

Tactical asset allocation involves adjusting a portfolio's asset mix based on short-term market forecasts or investment opportunities. ETFs are ideal for this strategy due to their flexibility and ease of trading.

**Advantages**:
- **Swift Adjustments**: ETFs allow investors to quickly change their exposure to different asset classes or sectors in response to market conditions.
- **Cost-Effectiveness**: Lower transaction costs compared to individual securities make ETFs a cost-effective choice for tactical shifts.

**Example**: During a market downturn, an investor might increase their allocation to defensive sectors using sector-specific ETFs.

### Cash Management

ETFs play a crucial role in managing cash within an investment portfolio. They allow investors to park money temporarily while seeking long-term opportunities.

**Benefits**:
- **Liquidity**: ETFs can be easily bought and sold, providing quick access to cash.
- **Flexibility**: Investors can use ETFs to maintain market exposure while deciding on future investments.

**Example**: A Canadian investor might use a short-term bond ETF to manage cash reserves, benefiting from liquidity and modest returns.

### Exposure to Hard-to-Access Markets

ETFs provide access to niche or previously inaccessible markets, such as emerging markets, specific sectors, or specialized investment themes.

**Diversification Benefits**:
- **Emerging Markets**: ETFs offer exposure to high-growth regions with potentially higher returns.
- **Sector-Specific Opportunities**: Investors can target sectors like clean energy or technology.

**Example**: A Canadian investor seeking exposure to Asian markets might use an ETF focused on the MSCI Asia ex-Japan Index.

### Tax Loss Harvesting

Tax loss harvesting involves selling securities at a loss to offset capital gains and reduce taxable income. ETFs can be utilized effectively for this purpose.

**Superficial Loss Rules**: Canadian tax regulations prevent investors from claiming losses on the sale of an investment if they repurchase the same or identical investment within 30 days. ETFs can serve as substitute investments to maintain portfolio exposure while adhering to these rules.

**Example**: An investor might sell a Canadian equity ETF at a loss and purchase a similar but not identical ETF to maintain market exposure.

### Examples and Case Studies

**Case Study 1**: A Canadian pension fund uses the core and satellite strategy, with core holdings in broad market ETFs and satellite holdings in infrastructure and technology ETFs. This approach balances stability with targeted growth, enhancing overall portfolio performance.

**Case Study 2**: An individual investor employs tactical asset allocation, using ETFs to shift exposure between equities and bonds based on economic forecasts. This strategy allows for agile responses to market changes, optimizing returns.

### Glossary

- **Core Holdings**: Broad market ETFs that form the foundation of a diversified investment portfolio.
- **Satellite Holdings**: Specialized or sector-specific ETFs used to enhance portfolio returns or target specific investment themes.
- **Rebalancing**: The process of realigning the proportions of assets in a portfolio to maintain desired asset allocation.
- **Tactical Asset Allocation**: Adjusting the portfolio’s asset mix based on short-term market forecasts or investment opportunities.
- **Tax Loss Harvesting**: Selling securities at a loss to offset capital gains and reduce taxable income.
- **Superficial Loss Rules**: Tax regulations that prevent investors from claiming losses on the sale of an investment if they repurchase the same or identical investment within a short period.

### Conclusion

ETFs offer a versatile tool for implementing various investment strategies, each with its own set of benefits and applications. By understanding and leveraging these strategies, investors can enhance their portfolio performance, manage risk, and achieve their financial goals within the Canadian market.
