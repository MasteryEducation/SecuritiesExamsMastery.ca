## 20.3.3 Increasing Absolute Returns

In the realm of investment, the pursuit of absolute returns represents a strategic approach to achieving consistent portfolio growth, irrespective of market conditions. This section delves into the concept of absolute returns, explores strategies to achieve them, and highlights their benefits in enhancing overall portfolio performance and resilience.

### Concept of Absolute Returns

**Absolute Return** is defined as the return that an investment portfolio achieves, independent of market benchmarks or conditions. Unlike relative returns, which are measured against a specific index or benchmark, absolute returns focus solely on the positive gains generated by an investment. This approach is crucial for investors seeking consistent growth, as it emphasizes the importance of generating returns regardless of market fluctuations.

Absolute returns are particularly important in volatile markets, where traditional investments may underperform. By focusing on absolute returns, investors can aim for steady growth, providing a buffer against market downturns and enhancing the stability of their portfolios.

### Strategies for Absolute Returns

Alternative investments often focus on generating positive returns irrespective of market trends. Several strategies are employed to achieve absolute returns, with market-neutral and long/short equity strategies being among the most prominent.

#### Market-Neutral Strategy

A **Market-Neutral Strategy** seeks to minimize market risk by taking offsetting long and short positions. This strategy aims to profit from the relative price movements of securities, rather than relying on the overall direction of the market. By balancing long and short positions, market-neutral strategies can potentially generate positive returns even in declining markets.

For example, a Canadian hedge fund might employ a market-neutral strategy by taking long positions in undervalued stocks of Canadian banks like RBC and TD, while simultaneously shorting overvalued stocks in the same sector. This approach allows the fund to profit from the price discrepancies between the long and short positions, regardless of the broader market trends.

#### Long/Short Equity Strategy

The **Long/Short Equity Strategy** involves taking long positions in stocks expected to increase in value and short positions in stocks expected to decrease. This strategy provides flexibility and the potential for absolute returns by capitalizing on both rising and falling markets.

Consider a scenario where a Canadian investment firm identifies growth opportunities in the technology sector. The firm might take long positions in promising Canadian tech companies while shorting stocks of companies facing challenges. By doing so, the firm can generate returns from both the appreciation of long positions and the depreciation of short positions, aiming for absolute returns.

### Benefits of Absolute Returns

Achieving absolute returns can significantly enhance overall portfolio performance and resilience. Here are some key benefits:

- **Consistent Growth:** Absolute return strategies aim for steady gains, providing a reliable source of growth even in volatile markets.
- **Risk Mitigation:** By focusing on positive returns regardless of market conditions, absolute return strategies can reduce the impact of market downturns on a portfolio.
- **Diversification:** Incorporating absolute return strategies into a portfolio adds a layer of diversification, reducing reliance on traditional asset classes.
- **Enhanced Resilience:** Portfolios with absolute return strategies are often more resilient to market shocks, maintaining stability during economic uncertainties.

### Examples of Absolute Return Strategies

To illustrate the effectiveness of absolute return strategies, let's explore some real-world examples:

#### Case Study 1: Canadian Pension Fund

A major Canadian pension fund implemented a market-neutral strategy to enhance its portfolio's resilience. By taking long positions in undervalued Canadian equities and short positions in overvalued ones, the fund achieved positive returns during a period of market volatility. This approach not only protected the fund from market downturns but also contributed to its long-term growth.

#### Case Study 2: Hedge Fund Success

A Canadian hedge fund specializing in long/short equity strategies successfully navigated the 2020 market turbulence. By identifying opportunities in the healthcare and renewable energy sectors, the fund generated substantial returns from both long and short positions. This strategy allowed the fund to outperform traditional benchmarks and deliver absolute returns to its investors.

### Glossary

- **Absolute Return:** The return that an investment portfolio achieves regardless of market benchmarks or conditions.
- **Market-Neutral Strategy:** An investment strategy that seeks to avoid some form of market risk by taking offsetting long and short positions.
- **Long/Short Equity Strategy:** An investment strategy that involves taking long positions in stocks expected to increase in value and short positions in stocks expected to decrease.

### Conclusion

Increasing absolute returns through alternative investment strategies offers a compelling approach to achieving consistent portfolio growth and resilience. By employing market-neutral and long/short equity strategies, investors can navigate market volatility and enhance their portfolios' performance. As the financial landscape continues to evolve, understanding and implementing absolute return strategies will be crucial for investors seeking to optimize their investment outcomes.
