## 22.6 Differentiate Among the Types of Income Trusts

Income trusts are a unique and significant component of the Canadian investment landscape, offering investors a way to gain exposure to income-generating assets. This section will explore the different types of income trusts, their advantages and disadvantages, and their taxation implications, providing a comprehensive understanding for investors and financial professionals.

### Definition and Comparison

**Income Trusts** are investment vehicles that hold and operate income-generating assets, distributing the income to unitholders. Unlike closed-end funds and mutual funds, which primarily focus on capital appreciation, income trusts emphasize generating steady income through their underlying assets, which can include real estate, businesses, or commodities.

- **Closed-End Funds**: These are investment funds with a fixed number of shares that trade on stock exchanges. They are primarily focused on capital gains and may distribute income, but not to the extent of income trusts.
- **Mutual Funds**: These are open-ended investment funds that pool money from many investors to purchase securities. They offer liquidity and diversification but are generally more focused on growth rather than income.

Income trusts are particularly appealing to income-focused investors due to their structure, which allows for regular income distributions.

### Types of Income Trusts

#### Real Estate Investment Trusts (REITs)

**Real Estate Investment Trusts (REITs)** invest in income-producing real estate properties, such as shopping malls, office buildings, and residential complexes. REITs are required to distribute a significant portion of their income to unitholders, making them attractive for investors seeking regular income.

- **Structure**: REITs pool capital from investors to purchase and manage real estate properties. They generate income through rent and property appreciation.
- **Income Distribution**: By law, REITs must distribute at least 90% of their taxable income to maintain their tax-efficient status, providing consistent income to investors.

#### Business Trusts

**Business Trusts** hold and operate businesses across various sectors, such as energy, utilities, and manufacturing. These trusts distribute income generated from the business operations to investors.

- **Structure**: Business trusts own and manage businesses, generating income through operational profits.
- **Sector Exposure**: They offer exposure to different sectors, providing diversification benefits to investors.

#### Commodity Pools and Business Trusts

While both commodity pools and business trusts involve pooling investor capital, they differ in their focus and operations.

- **Commodity Pools**: These are investment funds that pool capital to invest in commodity futures and options. They are more speculative and focus on capital gains rather than income.
- **Business Trusts**: Focus on generating income through operating businesses, offering a more stable income stream compared to the speculative nature of commodity pools.

### Advantages of Income Trusts

#### High Yield Potential

Income trusts are known for their high yield potential, making them attractive to income-focused investors. The regular income distributions can be particularly appealing in a low-interest-rate environment.

#### Diversification

Income trusts provide diversification by offering exposure to various asset classes and sectors. For example, REITs offer real estate exposure, while business trusts provide access to different industries.

#### Tax Efficiency

Income trusts can offer tax advantages, especially when held in registered accounts like RRSPs or TFSAs. The income distributions may be more tax-efficient compared to dividends from taxable Canadian corporations.

### Disadvantages and Risks

#### Market and Economic Risks

Income trusts are exposed to market fluctuations and economic cycles, which can impact the value of the underlying assets and income distributions. For instance, a downturn in the real estate market can affect REITs' income.

#### Trading Price Divergence

Income trusts may trade at discounts or premiums to their Net Asset Value (NAV), affecting investor returns. This divergence can be influenced by market sentiment and liquidity.

#### Operational Dependence

The performance of income trusts is heavily dependent on the management and operation of the underlying assets. Poor management or operational challenges can negatively impact income distributions and trust value.

### Taxation of Income Trusts

Income trusts are taxed differently from taxable Canadian corporations, offering potential tax benefits to investors.

- **Taxable Canadian Corporations**: Pay corporate taxes on profits before distributing dividends to shareholders, who then pay taxes on the dividends.
- **Income Trusts**: Distribute income directly to unitholders, who are taxed on the income received. This can be more tax-efficient, especially for REITs, which benefit from special tax rules allowing income distributions to be tax-efficient in registered accounts.

### Glossary

- **Income Trust**: An investment vehicle holding and operating income-generating assets, traded on stock exchanges similar to closed-end funds.
- **Real Estate Investment Trust (REIT)**: A trust that invests in income-producing real estate and distributes most of its income to unitholders.
- **Business Trust**: A trust that owns and operates businesses across various sectors, distributing income to investors.
- **Net Asset Value (NAV)**: The per-share value of a fund’s assets minus its liabilities.
- **Tax Efficiency**: The ability to minimize tax liabilities through strategic investment selections and account placements.
- **Market Risk**: The risk of losses due to factors affecting the overall financial markets.
- **Economic Risk**: The risk of losses due to changes in economic conditions affecting the underlying assets.

### Conclusion

Income trusts offer a unique investment opportunity for those seeking regular income and diversification. Understanding the different types of income trusts, their advantages, risks, and tax implications is crucial for making informed investment decisions. By leveraging the benefits of income trusts, investors can enhance their portfolios with steady income streams and diversified exposure to various asset classes.
