## 22.8 Differentiate Among the Types of Income Trusts

Income trusts are a unique type of pooled investment vehicle that offer investors the opportunity to earn income from a diversified portfolio of assets. Similar to closed-end funds, income trusts are publicly traded on stock exchanges, providing investors with liquidity and access to a variety of asset classes. In Canada, the two primary types of income trusts are Real Estate Investment Trusts (REITs) and Business Trusts. This section will explore the characteristics, advantages, risks, and tax considerations of each type, providing a comprehensive understanding of their roles in the investment landscape.

### Overview and Classification

Income trusts are structured to hold income-generating assets and distribute the majority of their income to unitholders. This structure allows investors to benefit from regular income distributions, often with tax advantages. The main types of income trusts are:

- **Real Estate Investment Trusts (REITs):** These trusts invest in income-producing real estate properties and distribute rental income to unitholders.
- **Business Trusts:** These trusts hold and operate businesses across various industries, distributing income generated from their operations to investors.

### Real Estate Investment Trusts (REITs)

#### Definition and Structure

Real Estate Investment Trusts (REITs) are investment vehicles that own, operate, or finance income-producing real estate. They provide investors with the opportunity to invest in a diversified portfolio of real estate assets without the need to directly purchase or manage properties. REITs specialize in various sectors, including shopping centers, senior housing, office buildings, industrial properties, and residential rentals. By pooling capital from multiple investors, REITs can acquire and manage a broad range of properties, distributing the rental income generated to unitholders.

#### Advantages of REITs

1. **Diversification:** REITs offer investors access to a diversified portfolio of properties across different sectors, reducing the risk associated with investing in a single property or market.

2. **Income Generation:** REITs are required to distribute a significant portion of their income to unitholders, often resulting in high yield potential. This makes them an attractive option for income-seeking investors.

3. **Liquidity:** Unlike direct real estate investments, REIT units can be bought and sold on stock exchanges, providing investors with higher liquidity and flexibility.

#### Risks and Disadvantages

1. **Market and Economic Risks:** REITs are exposed to fluctuations in the real estate market and broader economic cycles, which can impact property values and rental income.

2. **Interest Rate Sensitivity:** Rising interest rates can negatively affect REIT valuations and increase borrowing costs, impacting their profitability.

3. **Management Risk:** The performance of a REIT is heavily dependent on the expertise and decisions of its management team, which can introduce additional risk.

#### Tax Considerations

REITs are generally structured to be tax-efficient, with income distributions often eligible for registered accounts like Registered Retirement Savings Plans (RRSPs) and Registered Retirement Income Funds (RRIFs). This allows investors to defer taxes on income until withdrawal, enhancing the tax efficiency of their investment.

#### Glossary

- **Real Estate Investment Trust (REIT):** A trust that invests in income-producing real estate and distributes most of its income to unitholders.
- **Liquidity:** The ease with which an asset can be bought or sold in the market without affecting its price.
- **Yield:** The income returned on an investment, typically expressed as an annual percentage.
- **Interest Rate Sensitivity:** The degree to which a fund’s performance is affected by changes in interest rates.
- **Diversification:** The practice of spreading investments across various assets to reduce risk.

### Business Trusts

#### Definition and Structure

Business Trusts are investment vehicles that hold and operate business assets across various industries. These trusts distribute income generated from their operations to investors, similar to closed-end funds. Business trusts can encompass a wide range of underlying assets, including natural gas processing facilities, restaurant chains, and manufacturing operations. This diversity allows investors to gain exposure to multiple industries through a single investment vehicle.

#### Advantages of Business Trusts

1. **Income Generation:** Business trusts often provide high income distributions from their operating assets, appealing to investors seeking regular income.

2. **Diversification:** By investing in a business trust, investors gain exposure to multiple industries and sectors, reducing the risk associated with investing in a single business or industry.

3. **Liquidity:** Like REITs, business trust units can be traded on stock exchanges, offering investors liquidity and the ability to adjust their investment positions as needed.

#### Risks and Disadvantages

1. **Market and Economic Risks:** Business trusts are subject to the operational performance of their underlying businesses and overall market conditions, which can impact income distributions and unit prices.

2. **Trading Price Divergence:** The trading prices of business trust units can deviate from their net asset value (NAV), leading to risks associated with buying at premiums or selling at discounts.

3. **Operational Dependence:** The success of a business trust is reliant on the management performance of the underlying businesses, introducing additional risk factors.

#### Tax Considerations

Income trusts, including business trusts, are taxed similarly to taxable Canadian corporations. The income distributed to investors is taxable, and investors should be aware of the tax implications when considering these investments.

#### Glossary

- **Business Trust:** A trust that holds and operates business assets, distributing income to investors similar to closed-end funds.
- **Trading Price Divergence:** The phenomenon where trading prices deviate from the net asset value of the underlying assets.
- **Operational Performance:** The effectiveness of management in running the underlying businesses and generating income.
- **Market Condition:** The current state of the financial markets, affecting the performance of business trusts.

### Conclusion

Income trusts, including REITs and Business Trusts, offer investors unique opportunities to earn income from diversified portfolios of assets. By understanding the structure, advantages, risks, and tax considerations of each type, investors can make informed decisions that align with their financial goals. Whether seeking high income, diversification, or liquidity, income trusts can play a valuable role in a well-rounded investment strategy.
