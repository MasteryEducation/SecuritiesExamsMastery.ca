## 21.2 Identify the Strategies Most Likely to Be Used in Alternative Mutual Funds

Alternative mutual funds, often referred to as "liquid alts," have gained popularity for their ability to offer retail investors access to sophisticated investment strategies traditionally reserved for hedge funds. These funds aim to provide diversification, risk management, and potential for enhanced returns. In this section, we will delve into the strategies most likely to be used in alternative mutual funds, focusing on regulatory considerations, suitable strategies, and practical examples within the Canadian financial landscape.

### Regulatory Considerations

Understanding the regulatory framework governing alternative mutual funds is crucial for both fund managers and investors. In Canada, these funds are subject to specific regulations that dictate their operations, including leverage limits, derivatives usage, and liquidity requirements. The Canadian Investment Regulatory Organization (CIRO) and provincial regulators ensure that these funds maintain transparency and protect investors.

- **Leverage:** Alternative mutual funds are permitted to use leverage, but it is typically capped to prevent excessive risk-taking. This allows funds to amplify returns while maintaining a controlled risk profile.
- **Derivatives Usage:** Derivatives are commonly used for hedging and speculative purposes. However, their use is regulated to ensure that funds do not expose investors to undue risk.
- **Liquidity Requirements:** These funds must maintain a level of liquidity that allows for daily net asset value (NAV) calculations and redemptions, ensuring investors can access their capital when needed.

### Suitable Strategies for Liquid Alts

Given the regulatory constraints, alternative mutual funds must employ strategies that align with liquidity and NAV calculation requirements. Here are some of the primary strategies used:

#### Managed Futures

Managed futures involve trading futures contracts to profit from market trends. These strategies are highly liquid and can be adjusted quickly to respond to market changes. Managed futures funds typically employ systematic trading models to capture trends across various asset classes, including commodities, currencies, and interest rates.

#### Equity Market-Neutral

Equity market-neutral strategies aim to achieve market neutrality by balancing long and short equity positions. This approach seeks to profit from stock selection rather than market movements, making it suitable for volatile markets. By maintaining a net zero market exposure, these funds can potentially generate returns regardless of market direction.

#### Long/Short Equity

Long/short equity strategies involve taking long positions in undervalued stocks while shorting overvalued ones. This approach allows fund managers to capture alpha through stock selection while hedging against market downturns. The flexibility to adjust long and short exposures makes this strategy adaptable to changing market conditions.

#### Global Macro

Global macro strategies are based on macroeconomic trends across global markets. Fund managers analyze economic indicators, geopolitical events, and monetary policies to make investment decisions. This strategy often involves a diverse range of asset classes, including equities, bonds, currencies, and commodities, providing broad diversification.

#### Dedicated Short Bias

Dedicated short bias strategies maintain a net short exposure to the market. These funds aim to profit from declining markets by shorting overvalued securities. While this strategy can be risky in rising markets, it provides valuable diversification and downside protection in bearish conditions.

#### Merger (Risk) Arbitrage

Merger arbitrage strategies seek to profit from merger and acquisition deals. Fund managers analyze announced deals and invest in the target company's stock, betting on the successful completion of the transaction. This strategy can offer attractive risk-adjusted returns, particularly in stable market environments.

#### Fixed-Income Arbitrage

Fixed-income arbitrage strategies target pricing inefficiencies in fixed-income securities. These funds exploit discrepancies in interest rates, yield curves, and credit spreads to generate returns. The strategy requires sophisticated modeling and risk management to navigate interest rate and credit risks effectively.

### Exclusion of Illiquid Strategies

While alternative mutual funds offer access to a range of strategies, certain illiquid strategies are generally unsuitable due to liquidity and NAV measurement challenges. Strategies like private equity and equity real estate require long-term capital commitments and are not conducive to the liquidity requirements of mutual funds.

### Strategy Selection

Alternative mutual fund managers must carefully select strategies that balance return potential with liquidity and regulatory compliance. This involves rigorous analysis of market conditions, risk factors, and investor needs. Managers often employ a combination of strategies to achieve diversification and optimize risk-adjusted returns.

### Examples of Strategies in Liquid Alts

To illustrate how these strategies are implemented, let's consider two examples:

#### Managed Futures

A Canadian alternative mutual fund might use managed futures to capitalize on commodity price trends. By employing a systematic trading model, the fund can quickly adjust its positions in response to market signals, capturing profits from both rising and falling markets. The fund's liquidity allows for daily NAV calculations and investor redemptions.

#### Equity Market-Neutral

An equity market-neutral fund in Canada might balance long positions in undervalued Canadian stocks with short positions in overvalued U.S. stocks. This approach aims to generate returns through stock selection while minimizing exposure to broader market movements. The fund's market-neutral stance provides stability in volatile conditions.

### Glossary

- **Liquid Alts:** Alternative mutual funds offering liquid access to alternative strategies with mutual fund-like features.
- **Managed Futures:** Investment strategies using futures contracts to profit from market trends.
- **Equity Market-Neutral:** Strategies aiming for market neutrality through balanced long and short equity positions.
- **Long/Short Equity:** Strategies taking both long and short positions to capture alpha from stock selection.
- **Global Macro:** Strategies based on macroeconomic trends across global markets.
- **Dedicated Short Bias:** Strategies maintaining a net short exposure to the market.
- **Merger (Risk) Arbitrage:** Strategies profiting from merger and acquisition deals.
- **Fixed-Income Arbitrage:** Strategies targeting pricing inefficiencies in fixed-income securities.

### Conclusion

Alternative mutual funds offer a unique opportunity for investors to access sophisticated investment strategies within a regulated framework. By understanding the regulatory considerations and suitable strategies, investors can make informed decisions that align with their financial goals. As the Canadian financial landscape continues to evolve, alternative mutual funds will play an increasingly important role in portfolio diversification and risk management.
