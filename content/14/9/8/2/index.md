## 21.8.2 Focused on Specific Objectives

In the realm of alternative investments, particularly liquid alternatives (liquid alts), having clear and specific investment objectives is paramount. These objectives guide investors in selecting appropriate strategies that align with their broader portfolio goals. This section delves into the significance of defining specific objectives, aligning them with portfolio goals, and the benefits of such focused strategies.

### Defining Specific Objectives

The foundation of any successful investment strategy is the establishment of clear, specific objectives. When it comes to liquid alts, these objectives often revolve around diversification, risk management, and capital preservation.

- **Diversification:** This involves spreading investments across various asset classes to reduce risk. Liquid alts can provide exposure to asset classes that are typically uncorrelated with traditional stocks and bonds, thereby enhancing portfolio diversification.
  
- **Risk Management:** This is the process of identifying, assessing, and prioritizing risks, followed by coordinated efforts to minimize, monitor, and control the probability or impact of unfortunate events. Liquid alts can offer strategies that hedge against market volatility and protect against downside risks.

- **Capital Preservation:** This strategy focuses on maintaining the initial capital while achieving modest returns. Liquid alts can be structured to preserve capital by employing conservative strategies that prioritize stability over high returns.

### Alignment with Portfolio Goals

Liquid alts can be tailored to meet specific investment goals within a broader portfolio context. By aligning these investments with overall portfolio objectives, investors can achieve targeted risk-return profiles.

- **Role of Alternative Strategies:** Alternative strategies, such as hedge funds, private equity, and real assets, can be integrated into a portfolio to achieve specific outcomes. For instance, a Canadian investor might use liquid alts to hedge against currency risk or to gain exposure to commodities that are less correlated with the Canadian equity market.

- **Targeted Risk-Return Profiles:** By incorporating liquid alts, investors can adjust their portfolios to achieve desired risk-return characteristics. For example, a conservative investor might focus on capital preservation and low volatility, while an aggressive investor might seek higher returns through more volatile strategies.

### Objective-Driven Strategies

Different alternative strategies align with specific investment objectives, allowing investors to tailor their approach based on their goals.

- **Managed Futures for Trend-Following:** Managed futures can be used to capture market trends and enhance return potential. These strategies involve trading futures contracts based on market trends, providing an opportunity to profit from both rising and falling markets.

- **Real Estate for Income and Diversification:** Investing in real estate through liquid alts can provide a steady income stream and diversification benefits. Real estate investments are often less correlated with traditional asset classes, offering a hedge against market volatility.

- **Hedge Funds for Absolute Returns:** Hedge funds aim to achieve absolute returns regardless of market conditions. By employing a variety of strategies, such as long/short equity or event-driven approaches, hedge funds can offer attractive risk-adjusted returns.

### Benefits of Focused Objectives

Having specific objectives facilitates better strategy selection and risk management. Well-defined investment goals provide clarity and direction, enabling investors to make informed decisions.

- **Strategy Selection:** With clear objectives, investors can choose strategies that best align with their goals. For example, an investor focused on diversification might select a multi-strategy liquid alt fund that offers exposure to various asset classes.

- **Risk Management:** Specific objectives help investors assess and manage risks more effectively. By understanding their risk tolerance and investment horizon, investors can select strategies that align with their risk profile.

### Examples of Specific Objectives

1. **Enhance Portfolio Diversification:** By incorporating uncorrelated asset classes, investors can reduce portfolio volatility and improve risk-adjusted returns. For instance, adding commodities or real estate through liquid alts can provide diversification benefits.

2. **Achieve Absolute Returns:** Investors seeking absolute returns can employ diversified alternative strategies that aim to generate positive returns in any market environment. This might involve using hedge funds or managed futures to capture opportunities across different asset classes.

3. **Mitigate Portfolio Risk:** By incorporating risk-adjusted return measures, investors can focus on strategies that offer downside protection. This could involve using options or other derivatives to hedge against market downturns.

### Glossary

- **Diversification:** The practice of spreading investments across various assets to reduce risk.
- **Risk Management:** The process of identifying, assessing, and prioritizing risks followed by coordinated efforts to minimize, monitor, and control the probability or impact of unfortunate events.
- **Capital Preservation:** Investment strategy focused on maintaining the initial capital while achieving modest returns.

By focusing on specific objectives, investors can effectively incorporate liquid alts into their portfolios, enhancing diversification, managing risk, and preserving capital. These strategies provide the flexibility to adapt to changing market conditions and achieve desired investment outcomes.
