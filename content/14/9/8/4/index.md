## 21.8.4 Liquidity Needs

In the realm of alternative investments, understanding liquidity needs is crucial for aligning investment strategies with financial goals. Liquidity needs refer to the requirement to access invested capital within a certain timeframe without incurring significant losses. This section delves into how liquid alternatives (liquid alts) can meet these needs, balancing liquidity with strategic investment objectives, and influencing fund selection.

### Understanding Liquidity Needs

Liquidity needs are a fundamental consideration for investors, particularly those who may require access to their capital on short notice. These needs can arise from various circumstances, such as unexpected expenses, changes in financial goals, or market opportunities. The ability to liquidate investments without significant loss is essential for maintaining financial flexibility and stability.

### Role of Liquid Alts in Meeting Liquidity Needs

Liquid alts are designed to provide the benefits of alternative investments while offering the liquidity features of traditional mutual funds. They typically allow for daily redemptions, enabling investors to access their funds without significant penalties or delays. This daily liquidity is facilitated through mutual fund-like redemption features, making liquid alts suitable for investors with short- to medium-term liquidity requirements.

For example, a Canadian investor might choose a liquid alt fund that invests in managed futures or equity market-neutral strategies. These strategies can offer diversification and risk management benefits while maintaining the ability to redeem shares daily. This flexibility is particularly advantageous for investors who anticipate needing access to their capital within a few months to a few years.

### Balancing Liquidity and Strategy

While liquidity is important, it must be balanced with the pursuit of specific investment strategies and objectives. Investors should consider how their liquidity needs align with their overall financial goals and risk tolerance. Liquid alts offer a unique opportunity to achieve this balance by providing access to alternative strategies with transparent holdings and daily NAV (Net Asset Value) calculations.

Daily NAV calculations enable investors to track the value of their investments accurately and make informed decisions about when to redeem their shares. This transparency is crucial for managing liquidity effectively and ensuring that investment strategies remain aligned with financial objectives.

### Impact on Fund Selection

Investors' liquidity needs significantly influence the selection of appropriate alternative strategies. Those with higher liquidity requirements may favor liquid alts that offer consistent liquidity and ease of trading. For instance, funds that employ managed futures or equity market-neutral strategies often provide the necessary liquidity while still offering the potential for attractive returns and diversification benefits.

When selecting a fund, investors should consider factors such as the fund's redemption policies, historical performance, and the underlying assets' liquidity. By doing so, they can ensure that their chosen investment aligns with their liquidity needs and overall financial strategy.

### Examples

Consider a Canadian investor who anticipates needing access to their investment capital within the next year. This investor might choose a liquid alt fund that focuses on managed futures. Managed futures strategies typically involve trading futures contracts on various asset classes, providing diversification and the potential for positive returns in different market conditions. The daily liquidity feature of the fund allows the investor to redeem shares as needed, aligning with their short-term liquidity requirements.

Another example is an investor interested in equity market-neutral strategies, which aim to profit from both rising and falling stock prices while minimizing market risk. These strategies can offer consistent returns with lower volatility, making them suitable for investors with medium-term liquidity needs. The ability to redeem shares daily ensures that the investor can access their capital when necessary, without compromising their investment strategy.

### Glossary

- **Liquidity Needs:** The requirement to access invested capital within a certain timeframe.
- **Daily Liquidity:** The ability to redeem investments on a daily basis without significant penalties or delays.
- **NAV (Net Asset Value):** The value per share of a mutual fund or ETF on a specific date or time, enabling daily pricing.

By understanding and addressing liquidity needs, investors can make informed decisions about incorporating liquid alts into their portfolios. This approach allows for the pursuit of alternative investment strategies while maintaining the flexibility to access capital as needed.
