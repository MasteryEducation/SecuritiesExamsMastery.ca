## 21.8.3 Medium-Term Investment Horizon

In the realm of investment planning, understanding the concept of an investment horizon is crucial. The medium-term investment horizon, typically spanning 3 to 10 years, is a critical period for investors seeking to balance growth and risk management. This section delves into the strategic use of liquid alternatives (liquid alts) within this timeframe, highlighting their potential to enhance portfolio stability and growth.

### Definition and Importance

A **medium-term investment horizon** refers to an investment perspective that extends over several years, generally between 3 and 10 years. This period is significant for investors who aim to achieve a balance between capital appreciation and risk mitigation. Liquid alternatives are particularly well-suited for this horizon due to their ability to provide consistent returns and manage risk effectively.

Liquid alts, which include strategies like hedge funds, real estate, and commodities, offer a unique blend of liquidity and alternative investment benefits. Their adaptability to changing market conditions makes them an attractive option for medium-term investors who seek to navigate market volatility while pursuing growth.

### Alignment with Alternative Strategies

Medium-term horizons align well with the performance characteristics of various alternative strategies. For instance, **trend-following strategies** capitalize on market momentum, making them suitable for capturing medium-term gains. These strategies can identify and exploit market trends over several years, providing investors with opportunities for substantial returns.

Similarly, **market-neutral strategies** aim to reduce market risk by balancing long and short positions. This approach can be particularly beneficial for medium-term investors, as it allows them to achieve steady returns regardless of market direction. By incorporating these strategies, investors can enhance their portfolios' resilience against market fluctuations.

### Investment Planning

Investors with medium-term horizons can integrate liquid alts to complement their long-term investments and manage volatility. For example, a Canadian investor might allocate a portion of their portfolio to liquid alts like real estate investment trusts (REITs) or commodity-focused funds. These investments can provide diversification benefits and act as a hedge against inflation, enhancing overall portfolio stability.

Consider a scenario where an investor holds a diversified portfolio of Canadian equities and bonds. By adding a liquid alt strategy focused on global macroeconomic trends, the investor can mitigate domestic market risks and tap into international growth opportunities. This approach not only diversifies the portfolio but also positions it to benefit from global economic shifts.

### Examples

To illustrate the practical application of liquid alts in a medium-term investment strategy, let's consider a case study involving a Canadian pension fund. The fund aims to achieve stable returns over a 5-year period while minimizing exposure to market downturns. By incorporating a mix of liquid alts, including long/short equity funds and managed futures, the fund can achieve its objectives.

In another example, a medium-term investor might use a market-neutral strategy to balance their portfolio during periods of economic uncertainty. By maintaining a mix of long and short positions, the investor can protect their capital while still participating in market upswings.

### Benefits for Medium-Term Investors

Liquid alts offer several benefits for medium-term investors, including:

- **Adaptability to Market Conditions:** Liquid alts can adjust to changing market environments, providing a protective buffer during downturns.
- **Diversification:** By incorporating a variety of asset classes and strategies, liquid alts enhance portfolio diversification, reducing overall risk.
- **Consistent Returns:** Many liquid alt strategies are designed to deliver steady returns over the medium term, aligning with investors' goals for growth and stability.

### Glossary

- **Investment Horizon:** The length of time an investor expects to hold an investment before taking cash out.
- **Medium-Term Horizon:** An investment perspective spanning several years, suitable for strategies with moderate risk and return profiles.

By understanding and leveraging the unique characteristics of liquid alternatives, medium-term investors can effectively navigate the complexities of the financial markets, achieving a balance between risk and return that aligns with their investment goals.
