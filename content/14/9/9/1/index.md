## 21.9.1 Comprehensive Due Diligence Process

Investing in alternative strategy funds requires a meticulous due diligence process to ensure that the investment aligns with your financial goals and risk tolerance. This section provides a detailed, step-by-step guide to conducting comprehensive due diligence, focusing on key areas such as the investment management organization, risk analysis, operations, fund structure, and performance evaluation. By following these steps, investors can make informed decisions and mitigate potential risks associated with alternative investments.

### Step-by-Step Process

#### 1. Structure of the Investment Management Organization

Understanding the structure of the investment management organization is crucial for assessing its credibility and stability.

- **Firm's History and Ownership Structure:**
  - Investigate the firm's background, including its founding date, ownership changes, and any significant milestones.
  - Assess the regulatory registration and compliance with Canadian financial regulations, such as those set by the Canadian Investment Regulatory Organization (CIRO).

- **Key Personnel and Management Team:**
  - Evaluate the experience and qualifications of the management team and key personnel.
  - Consider their track record in managing similar funds and their reputation in the industry.

#### 2. Investment Management Information

A thorough review of the fund's investment management information provides insights into its strategy and potential for success.

- **Assets Under Management (AUM):**
  - Analyze the growth of AUM over the past five years to gauge the fund's market acceptance and stability.

- **Investment Strategies:**
  - Examine the uniqueness of the fund’s strategies and their alignment with performance claims.
  - Ensure that the strategies are well-documented and transparent.

#### 3. Risk Analysis

Risk management is a critical component of due diligence, particularly for alternative investments.

- **Risk Management Frameworks:**
  - Evaluate the fund’s policies for diversification, leverage, and derivatives usage.
  - Assess the robustness of the risk management framework, including stress testing and hedging capabilities.

- **Handling Risk Scenarios:**
  - Review the fund’s ability to manage various risk scenarios and its historical performance during market downturns.

#### 4. Operations

Operational efficiency and reliability are essential for the smooth functioning of a fund.

- **Service Providers:**
  - Analyze the reputation and reliability of service providers such as prime brokers, custodians, and administrators.
  - Ensure that these providers have a strong track record and are compliant with regulatory standards.

- **Operational Infrastructure:**
  - Evaluate the systems in place for trading, compliance, and record-keeping.
  - Assess the fund’s ability to adapt to technological advancements and regulatory changes.

#### 5. Fund Structure

The structure of the fund has significant implications for investors.

- **Form of the Offering:**
  - Examine the fund’s legal structure and its implications for liquidity, tax treatment, and investor rights.

- **Redemption Terms and Fee Structures:**
  - Assess the fund’s redemption terms, fee structures, and any restrictions or conditions.
  - Ensure that these terms are transparent and fair to investors.

#### 6. Investment Performance

Analyzing the fund’s historical performance is crucial for understanding its potential future returns.

- **Performance Metrics:**
  - Review historical performance metrics, including returns, volatility, and risk-adjusted ratios.
  - Compare the fund’s performance against relevant benchmarks and peer groups to assess its competitiveness.

#### 7. Account Structure and Composition

Understanding the fund’s account structure provides insights into its investor base and stability.

- **Distribution of Assets:**
  - Review the distribution of assets among different investor types, such as individuals and institutions.
  - Assess the concentration of assets among top investors and the implications for fund stability.

#### 8. Fees

Fees can significantly impact net returns and should be carefully scrutinized.

- **Management and Performance Fees:**
  - Scrutinize the management fee, performance fee, and any other costs associated with investing in the fund.
  - Evaluate how these fees impact net returns and the alignment of interests between managers and investors.

### Evaluation Criteria

- **Verification of Managerial Claims:**
  - Verify managerial claims through independent sources to ensure accuracy and reliability.
  - Emphasize the importance of transparency in reporting and accessibility of performance data.

- **Operational Robustness:**
  - Highlight the significance of operational robustness and the role of reliable service providers in ensuring fund stability.

### Examples

#### Hypothetical Case Study: Canadian Pension Fund

Consider a Canadian pension fund evaluating an alternative strategy fund. The pension fund conducts due diligence by:

- Reviewing the fund’s investment strategy and alignment with its long-term goals.
- Analyzing the fund’s historical performance during economic downturns.
- Verifying the fund’s risk management practices through independent audits.

Based on these findings, the pension fund decides whether to invest, demonstrating how due diligence influences investment decisions.

### Glossary

- **Investment Committee:** A group responsible for making investment decisions and overseeing portfolio management.
- **Risk Management Framework:** The overall structure of policies and procedures that a fund uses to identify, assess, and mitigate risks.
- **Performance Metrics:** Quantitative measures used to evaluate the performance of an investment fund.
- **Fee Structure:** The arrangement of fees charged by the fund, including management and performance fees.
- **Stress Testing:** Assessing how a fund’s portfolio would perform under extreme market conditions.
