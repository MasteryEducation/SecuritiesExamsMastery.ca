## 21.5 Describe the Due Diligence Process That Should Be Conducted When Contemplating Investment in an Alternative Strategy Fund

Investing in alternative strategy funds can offer diversification and potentially higher returns, but it also comes with unique risks and complexities. A comprehensive due diligence process is essential to mitigate these risks and ensure that the investment aligns with an investor's objectives and risk tolerance. This section outlines a detailed framework for conducting due diligence on alternative strategy funds, focusing on key areas of inquiry, risk analysis, and performance evaluation.

### Comprehensive Due Diligence Framework

The due diligence process for alternative strategy funds involves a thorough evaluation of several critical areas. This framework helps investors make informed decisions by assessing the fund's suitability and identifying potential risks.

#### Key Areas of Inquiry

1. **Investment Manager Evaluation**
   - **Background and Experience:** Investigate the investment manager's history, including their track record, qualifications, and reputation in the industry.
   - **Governance and Compensation:** Understand the governance structure and compensation arrangements to assess alignment of interests.
   - **Team Stability and Expertise:** Evaluate the stability and expertise of the management team, including their personal investments in the fund.

2. **Strategy Assessment**
   - **Investment Objectives and Strategies:** Examine the fund's objectives and principal strategies, noting any changes over time.
   - **Risk Management Frameworks:** Assess the robustness of the fund's risk management practices.
   - **Performance History:** Review historical performance data to evaluate consistency and risk-adjusted returns.

3. **Operations Evaluation**
   - **Service Providers:** Assess the reputation and reliability of service providers such as prime brokers, custodians, administrators, auditors, and legal counsel.
   - **Operational Infrastructure:** Evaluate the fund's systems, record-keeping, and compliance practices.

4. **Risk Analysis**
   - **Risk Identification and Management:** Examine how the fund identifies, quantifies, and manages risks.
   - **Stress Testing:** Review the fund's stress testing procedures and exposure to various risk factors.

5. **Investment Performance Evaluation**
   - **Historical Performance:** Analyze the fund's performance, including consistency, drawdowns, and risk-adjusted returns.
   - **Benchmark Comparison:** Compare the fund's performance against appropriate benchmarks and peer groups.

6. **Fee Structure Analysis**
   - **Fee Scrutiny:** Examine the fee structure, including management fees, performance fees, and other costs.
   - **Impact on Returns:** Assess how fees affect net returns and the alignment of interests between managers and investors.

### Comprehensive Due Diligence Process

A structured approach to due diligence involves eight critical steps:

1. **Structure of the Investment Management Organization**
   - Evaluate the organizational structure, including ownership, governance, and decision-making processes.

2. **Investment Management Information**
   - Gather detailed information about the investment team, their experience, and their approach to managing the fund.

3. **Risk Analysis**
   - Conduct a thorough risk analysis, focusing on the fund's risk management framework and exposure to various risk factors.

4. **Operations**
   - Assess the operational capabilities of the fund, including technology, processes, and compliance.

5. **Fund Structure**
   - Review the legal and regulatory structure of the fund, including its domicile and regulatory compliance.

6. **Investment Performance**
   - Analyze historical performance data, focusing on consistency, drawdowns, and risk-adjusted returns.

7. **Account Structure and Composition**
   - Evaluate the composition of the fund's assets and liabilities, including liquidity and leverage.

8. **Fees**
   - Scrutinize the fee structure, including management fees, performance fees, and other costs.

### Examples and Case Studies

To illustrate the application of these due diligence questions and processes, consider the following hypothetical scenario:

**Case Study: Canadian Pension Fund Investment in an Alternative Strategy Fund**

A Canadian pension fund is considering investing in an alternative strategy fund managed by a well-known Canadian bank. The due diligence process involves:

- **Investment Manager Evaluation:** The pension fund reviews the bank's investment team, their track record, and governance structure. They find that the team has a strong history of managing similar funds with consistent returns.

- **Strategy Assessment:** The fund's investment objectives align with the pension fund's goals, and the risk management framework is robust. Historical performance data shows consistent risk-adjusted returns.

- **Operations Evaluation:** The bank's operational infrastructure is sound, with reliable service providers and strong compliance practices.

- **Risk Analysis:** The fund conducts regular stress tests and has a comprehensive risk management framework.

- **Investment Performance Evaluation:** The fund's performance is compared against relevant benchmarks, showing competitive returns.

- **Fee Structure Analysis:** The fee structure is transparent, with reasonable management and performance fees that align with the pension fund's interests.

### Glossary

- **Due Diligence:** A comprehensive appraisal of an investment undertaken by a prospective buyer to establish its assets, liabilities, and evaluate its potential.
- **Risk Management Framework:** The overall structure of policies and procedures that a fund uses to identify, assess, and mitigate risks.
- **Annual Information Form (AIF):** A document providing detailed information about an investment fund’s operations, used for regulatory compliance.
- **Prime Broker:** A financial institution providing services like lending securities and facilitating trades for hedge funds.
- **Custodian:** A financial institution that holds and safeguards a fund’s assets.
- **Commodity Pool Operator (CPO):** An entity that operates commodity pools and is regulated by the CFTC and NFA.
