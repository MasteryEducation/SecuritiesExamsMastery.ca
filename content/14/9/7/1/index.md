## 21.7.1 Comprehensive Due Diligence Process

Conducting a comprehensive due diligence process is crucial when contemplating investment in an alternative strategy fund. This process ensures that investors have a thorough understanding of the fund's structure, management, risks, operations, and performance. In this section, we will explore a step-by-step guide to conducting due diligence, emphasizing the importance of each component and providing practical examples to illustrate key concepts.

### Step-by-Step Process

#### 1. Structure of the Investment Management Organization

The first step in the due diligence process involves assessing the structure of the investment management organization. This includes:

- **Firm's History and Ownership Structure:** Understanding the firm's background, including its founding, ownership, and any significant changes over time, provides insights into its stability and long-term viability. For example, a firm with a long history and stable ownership may be more reliable than a newly established entity.

- **Regulatory Registration:** Verify the firm's registration with relevant regulatory bodies, such as the Canadian Investment Regulatory Organization (CIRO). This ensures compliance with industry standards and regulations.

- **Experience and Background of Key Personnel:** Evaluate the qualifications and track records of the management team and key personnel. Experienced managers with a history of successful fund management can be a positive indicator of future performance.

#### 2. Investment Management Information

Understanding the fund's investment management is critical to assessing its potential for success:

- **Assets Under Management (AUM):** Review the fund's AUM and its growth trajectory over the past five years. A consistent increase in AUM may indicate investor confidence and successful fund performance.

- **Investment Strategies:** Examine the fund's strategies, including their uniqueness and alignment with performance claims. For instance, a fund that claims to outperform the market should have a clear strategy that supports this claim.

#### 3. Risk Analysis

Risk management is a cornerstone of due diligence:

- **Risk Management Frameworks:** Evaluate the fund's policies for diversification, leverage, and derivatives usage. A robust risk management framework can mitigate potential losses.

- **Stress Testing and Hedging:** Assess the fund's ability to handle various risk scenarios through stress testing and hedging strategies. This can provide insights into how the fund might perform under adverse market conditions.

#### 4. Operations

Operational efficiency and reliability are crucial for fund success:

- **Service Providers:** Analyze the reliability and reputation of service providers, such as prime brokers, custodians, and administrators. Reputable service providers can enhance operational integrity.

- **Operational Infrastructure:** Evaluate the fund's systems for trading, compliance, and record-keeping. Efficient operations can reduce the risk of errors and enhance performance.

#### 5. Fund Structure

The structure of the fund can impact liquidity, tax treatment, and investor rights:

- **Offering Form:** Examine the form of the offering and its implications for investors. For example, a fund structured as a limited partnership may have different tax implications than a mutual fund.

- **Redemption Terms and Fee Structures:** Assess the fund's redemption terms, fee structures, and any restrictions or conditions. Understanding these elements can help investors make informed decisions.

#### 6. Investment Performance

Analyzing historical performance metrics is essential:

- **Performance Metrics:** Review the fund's returns, volatility, and risk-adjusted ratios. Compare these metrics against relevant benchmarks and peer groups to assess relative performance.

- **Case Study Example:** Consider a Canadian pension fund that consistently outperforms its benchmark. Analyzing its performance metrics can provide insights into its investment strategy and risk management practices.

#### 7. Account Structure and Composition

Understanding the distribution of assets among investors is important for assessing fund stability:

- **Investor Types:** Review the distribution of assets among different investor types, such as individuals and institutions. A diverse investor base can enhance fund stability.

- **Concentration of Assets:** Assess the concentration of assets among top investors. A high concentration may pose risks if a major investor withdraws funds.

#### 8. Fees

Fees can significantly impact net returns:

- **Fee Structure:** Scrutinize the management fee, performance fee, and any other costs associated with investing in the fund. Evaluate how these fees affect net returns and whether they align the interests of managers and investors.

### Evaluation Criteria

When conducting due diligence, it is important to:

- **Verify Managerial Claims:** Use independent sources to verify claims made by the fund's management. This ensures accuracy and transparency.

- **Transparency in Reporting:** Ensure that the fund provides transparent and accessible performance data. This allows investors to make informed decisions.

- **Operational Robustness:** Emphasize the significance of operational robustness and reliable service providers. Strong operations can enhance fund performance and reduce risks.

### Examples

To illustrate the due diligence process, consider the following hypothetical case study:

**Case Study: ABC Alternative Fund**

ABC Alternative Fund is a Canadian hedge fund with a unique investment strategy focused on renewable energy. During the due diligence process, an investor might:

- **Assess the Management Team:** Review the backgrounds of the fund's managers, noting their experience in renewable energy investments.

- **Evaluate Risk Management:** Examine the fund's use of derivatives to hedge against energy price fluctuations.

- **Analyze Performance Metrics:** Compare the fund's historical returns to a benchmark index of renewable energy stocks.

- **Review Fee Structure:** Consider the impact of the fund's performance fee on net returns.

Based on these findings, the investor can make an informed decision about whether to invest in ABC Alternative Fund.

### Glossary

- **Investment Committee:** A group responsible for making investment decisions and overseeing portfolio management.
- **Risk Management Framework:** The overall structure of policies and procedures that a fund uses to identify, assess, and mitigate risks.
- **Performance Metrics:** Quantitative measures used to evaluate the performance of an investment fund.
- **Fee Structure:** The arrangement of fees charged by the fund, including management and performance fees.
- **Discovery Questions:** Specific inquiries made during due diligence to uncover critical information about the fund and management.
