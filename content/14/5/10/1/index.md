## 17.10.1 Benefits and Risks of Alternative Investments

Alternative investments have become an increasingly popular component of diversified portfolios, offering unique opportunities and challenges. This section delves into the benefits and risks associated with alternative investments, providing insights into their role within the Canadian financial landscape.

### Benefits of Alternative Investments

#### Enhanced Diversification

One of the primary benefits of alternative investments is their potential to enhance portfolio diversification. Unlike traditional asset classes such as stocks and bonds, alternative investments often exhibit low correlation with these markets. This low correlation can help reduce overall portfolio risk, as the performance of alternative investments may not be directly tied to the fluctuations of traditional markets.

For example, consider a Canadian pension fund that includes real estate and commodities in its portfolio. During periods of stock market volatility, these alternative assets may provide stability, as their values are influenced by different economic factors.

#### Potential for Higher Returns

Alternative investments can offer the potential for higher returns compared to traditional investments. This is often due to access to unique investment opportunities and strategies that are not available in conventional mutual funds. For instance, hedge funds may employ leverage and short-selling strategies to capitalize on market inefficiencies, potentially leading to superior returns.

A case study involving a Canadian hedge fund illustrates this point. By utilizing a long/short equity strategy, the fund was able to outperform the TSX Composite Index during a period of market downturn, demonstrating the potential for higher returns through alternative investment strategies.

#### Specialized Strategies

Alternative investments allow investors to participate in specialized strategies and niche markets. These strategies can include private equity, venture capital, and infrastructure investments, which are typically not accessible through traditional mutual funds. Such investments can provide exposure to innovative industries and emerging markets, offering growth opportunities that are not available in the public markets.

For example, a Canadian venture capital fund might invest in early-stage technology companies, providing investors with the opportunity to benefit from the growth of these companies before they go public.

#### Inflation Hedge

Certain alternative investments, such as real estate and commodities, can serve as an effective hedge against inflation. These assets tend to retain their intrinsic value over time, protecting investors from the eroding effects of inflation on purchasing power.

In Canada, real estate investments have historically provided a reliable inflation hedge. As property values and rental incomes tend to rise with inflation, real estate can offer a stable source of returns in an inflationary environment.

### Risks of Alternative Investments

#### Higher Volatility

While alternative investments offer potential benefits, they also come with higher volatility. The prices of alternative assets can experience significant swings, impacting the stability of a portfolio. This volatility can be attributed to factors such as market sentiment, economic conditions, and the inherent riskiness of certain alternative strategies.

For instance, a Canadian commodities fund may experience sharp price fluctuations due to changes in global supply and demand dynamics, geopolitical tensions, or currency fluctuations.

#### Liquidity Constraints

Many alternative investments are less liquid than traditional mutual funds, meaning they cannot be easily bought or sold without affecting their price. This lack of liquidity can pose challenges for investors who may need to access their funds quickly.

Private equity and real estate investments, for example, often require a long-term commitment, as they may take years to realize returns. Investors should be aware of these liquidity constraints and consider their investment horizon before committing to alternative assets.

#### Complexity

The sophisticated nature of alternative investment strategies requires a deeper understanding and ongoing monitoring. Investors must be well-versed in the complexities of these investments to make informed decisions.

For example, hedge funds often employ complex strategies involving derivatives and leverage, which can be difficult for the average investor to comprehend. This complexity necessitates a higher level of expertise and due diligence.

#### Regulatory Differences

Alternative investments may be subject to different or less stringent regulatory oversight compared to traditional mutual funds. This can lead to increased risks, as investors may not have the same level of protection.

In Canada, regulatory bodies such as the Investment Industry Regulatory Organization of Canada (IIROC) oversee certain types of alternative funds. However, the regulatory framework for alternative investments can vary, and investors should be aware of the specific regulations governing their investments.

#### Higher Fees

Alternative investments often come with higher expense ratios and performance fees, which can erode returns. These fees are typically higher than those associated with traditional mutual funds, reflecting the specialized nature of alternative investment strategies.

For instance, hedge funds commonly charge a management fee and a performance fee, which can significantly impact net returns. Investors should carefully consider the fee structures of alternative investments and assess their impact on overall performance.

### Regulatory Framework

Alternative investment funds in Canada must comply with specific regulations that differ from those governing traditional mutual funds. Understanding the regulatory framework is crucial for investors to ensure compliance and mitigate risks.

The IIROC, along with other regulatory bodies, oversees the activities of certain alternative funds. Investors should familiarize themselves with the regulations applicable to their investments and seek guidance from financial professionals when necessary.

### Guidelines for Investors

To navigate the complexities of alternative investments, investors should consider the following guidelines:

- **Educate Clients:** Ensure clients understand the unique characteristics and risk profiles of alternative investments. Providing comprehensive education can help clients make informed decisions aligned with their investment goals.

- **Assess Suitability:** Evaluate the suitability of alternative investments based on the client's overall risk tolerance and investment objectives. Consider factors such as investment horizon, liquidity needs, and risk appetite.

- **Understand Fee Structures:** Ensure clients are aware of the fee structures associated with alternative investments. Transparent communication about fees can help clients assess the true cost of their investments.

- **Consider Liquidity Aspects:** Discuss the liquidity constraints of alternative investments with clients, emphasizing the importance of aligning investment choices with their financial goals and timelines.

### Glossary

- **Liquidity:** The ease with which an investment can be bought or sold in the market without affecting its price.
- **Expense Ratio:** The annual cost expressed as a percentage of total assets that covers fund management and operating expenses.

By understanding the benefits and risks of alternative investments, investors can make informed decisions that align with their financial goals and risk tolerance. As the Canadian financial landscape continues to evolve, alternative investments offer opportunities for diversification and growth, albeit with certain challenges that require careful consideration.
