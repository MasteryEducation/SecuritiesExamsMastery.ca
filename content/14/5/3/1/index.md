## 17.3.1 Pricing Mutual Fund Units

Understanding how mutual fund units are priced is crucial for investors looking to make informed decisions in the Canadian financial market. This section delves into the calculation of the Net Asset Value per Share (NAVPS), the impact of sales loads, and the timing of pricing, providing a comprehensive guide to mutual fund pricing.

### Calculating NAVPS

The Net Asset Value per Share (NAVPS) is a fundamental metric used to determine the price of a mutual fund unit. It represents the per-unit value of the fund's assets minus its liabilities. The formula for calculating NAVPS is:

{{< katex >}}
\text{NAVPS} = \frac{\text{Total Assets} - \text{Total Liabilities}}{\text{Number of Units Outstanding}}
{{< /katex >}}

#### Example Calculation:

Consider a mutual fund with total assets of $13,000,000 and total liabilities of $1,000,000, with 1,000,000 units outstanding. The NAVPS would be calculated as follows:

{{< katex >}}
\text{NAVPS} = \frac{\$13,000,000 - \$1,000,000}{1,000,000 \text{ units}} = \$12 \text{ per unit}
{{< /katex >}}

#### Components of Total Assets and Liabilities:

- **Total Assets:** This includes the market value of the fund's securities, cash, and receivables. Securities are typically stocks, bonds, or other investments held by the fund. Cash includes any liquid assets, while receivables might include dividends or interest payments due to the fund.
  
- **Total Liabilities:** These are the expenses and fees the fund owes, such as management fees, administrative costs, and any other liabilities.

### Impact of Sales Loads

Sales loads are fees charged to investors when they buy or sell mutual fund units. These can significantly affect the amount invested and the returns received upon redemption.

#### Front-End Load Example:

A front-end load is a fee charged at the time of purchase. For instance, if an investor puts $1,000 into a mutual fund with a 4% front-end load, the amount actually invested is reduced:

- **Investment:** $1,000
- **Front-End Load:** 4%
- **Amount Invested:** $1,000 - ($1,000 \times 4\%) = $960

The offering price, considering the front-end load, is calculated as:

{{< katex >}}
\text{Offering Price} = \frac{100\% - \text{Sales Charge}}{100\%} \times \text{NAVPS} = \frac{96\%}{100\%} \times \$12 = \$11.52
{{< /katex >}}

#### Back-End Load Example:

A back-end load, or deferred sales charge, is applied when units are redeemed. For example, if an investor redeems units within four years and faces a 3% back-end load, the redemption price is:

- **NAVPS:** $15
- **Back-End Load:** 3%
- **Redemption Price:** 

{{< katex >}}
\text{Redemption Price} = \text{NAVPS} - (\text{NAVPS} \times \text{Back-End Load}) = \$15 - (\$15 \times 3\%) = \$14.55
{{< /katex >}}

### Timing of Pricing

The timing of mutual fund transactions is crucial, as it determines the NAVPS at which the transaction is executed:

- **Orders Placed Before 4:00 p.m. ET:** These are priced at the current day's NAVPS.
- **Orders Placed After 4:00 p.m. ET:** These are priced at the next business day's NAVPS.

This timing ensures that all investors are treated fairly, as the NAVPS reflects the most current valuation of the fund's assets.

### Guidelines for Investors

- **NAVPS Calculation:** Understanding how NAVPS is calculated helps investors assess the fair value of a mutual fund unit. It reflects the current portfolio value and ensures transparency in pricing.
  
- **Impact of Sales Loads:** Sales loads can significantly affect both the initial investment and the redemption amount. Investors should be aware of these charges and consider no-load funds if they wish to avoid such fees.

- **Order Timing:** Educating clients on the importance of order timing can help them optimize their investment transactions. Placing orders before the cut-off time ensures they benefit from the current day's pricing.

### Glossary

- **Redemption Fee:** A fee charged to investors for selling mutual fund shares before a specified period.
- **No-Load Fund:** A mutual fund that does not charge any sales commissions or loads upon purchase or redemption of shares.

### Conclusion

Pricing mutual fund units involves understanding NAVPS, sales loads, and the timing of transactions. By mastering these concepts, investors can make more informed decisions and optimize their investment strategies within the Canadian financial landscape.
