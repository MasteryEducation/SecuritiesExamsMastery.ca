## 17.6.1 Prohibited Selling Practices

In the realm of mutual fund sales, adhering to ethical standards and regulatory requirements is paramount. This section delves into the prohibited selling practices that financial professionals must avoid to maintain integrity and trust in the Canadian financial markets. Understanding these practices not only helps in compliance but also fosters a culture of transparency and accountability.

### Identifying and Avoiding Prohibited Practices

#### Backdating Orders

Backdating orders involve recording a purchase or redemption order with a date prior to the actual transaction date. This practice is prohibited as it can manipulate the pricing of mutual fund shares, potentially disadvantaging other investors. Mutual funds are priced based on the Net Asset Value per Share (NAVPS) at the close of each trading day. Therefore, orders must be processed according to the fund's pricing schedule to ensure fairness and accuracy.

**Example:** Consider a scenario where an investor places an order after the market closes but requests it to be backdated to take advantage of a lower NAVPS from the previous day. This would unfairly benefit the investor at the expense of others and is strictly prohibited.

#### Unauthorized Offers to Repurchase

Offering to repurchase mutual fund shares at prices other than the NAVPS without proper authorization is another prohibited practice. This can mislead investors about the true value of their investments and disrupt market equilibrium.

**Case Study:** A financial advisor promises to buy back shares from a client at a premium to the NAVPS to secure a sale. Such unauthorized offers can lead to regulatory penalties and damage the advisor's reputation.

#### Selling Unqualified Funds

Selling mutual funds that are not registered or approved in the client's province of residence is illegal. Each province in Canada has specific registration requirements, and selling unqualified funds can result in severe legal consequences.

**Practical Insight:** Always verify the registration status of a mutual fund before recommending it to clients. This ensures compliance with provincial regulations and protects both the advisor and the client.

#### Misleading Communications

Misleading communications involve making false statements or omitting critical information about a mutual fund's performance, risk, or fees. Such practices can deceive investors and lead to poor investment decisions.

**Example:** An advisor claims that a mutual fund has consistently outperformed the market without disclosing the associated risks or fees. This omission can mislead investors into believing the fund is a low-risk, high-return investment.

#### Guaranteeing Returns

Guaranteeing specific future returns or promising that a mutual fund will achieve certain performance targets is prohibited. Investment returns are inherently uncertain, and guaranteeing them can create unrealistic expectations.

**Real-World Scenario:** An advisor assures a client that a mutual fund will yield a 10% annual return. When the fund underperforms, the client may face financial losses and lose trust in the advisor.

### Implementing Ethical Selling Practices

To uphold ethical standards, financial professionals should:

- **Provide Accurate Information:** Ensure all communications about mutual funds are honest, complete, and transparent. This builds trust and helps clients make informed decisions.
  
- **Conduct Suitability Assessments:** Recommendations should be based on thorough assessments of the client's financial objectives and risk tolerance. This aligns investment choices with the client's needs and goals.

- **Maintain Professional Integrity:** Uphold regulatory standards in all client interactions, demonstrating a commitment to ethical practices and client welfare.

### Guidelines for Compliance

To prevent prohibited practices, financial institutions should:

- **Train and Educate:** Regularly train sales representatives on prohibited practices and ethical selling standards. This ensures they are well-informed and compliant with regulations.

- **Conduct Audits:** Perform periodic audits and compliance checks to identify and rectify any deviations from regulatory requirements.

- **Foster Transparency:** Encourage a culture of transparency and accountability within the sales team. This promotes ethical behavior and enhances client trust.

### Glossary

- **Misrepresentation:** Providing inaccurate or false information about a mutual fund to deceive investors.
  
- **NAVPS (Net Asset Value per Share):** The value per share of a mutual fund, used to price purchases and redemptions.
  
- **Transparency:** Openness in providing all necessary information to clients about mutual fund investments.

### Conclusion

Understanding and avoiding prohibited selling practices is crucial for maintaining ethical standards and regulatory compliance in mutual fund sales. By implementing ethical selling practices and fostering a culture of transparency, financial professionals can build trust with clients and contribute to the integrity of the Canadian financial markets.
