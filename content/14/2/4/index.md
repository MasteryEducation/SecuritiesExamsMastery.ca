## 14.4 Assessing Preferred Share Investment Quality

Preferred shares are a unique class of equity that offer investors a fixed dividend, often with priority over common stock dividends. Assessing the investment quality of preferred shares is crucial for investors seeking stable income and lower risk exposure. This section delves into the key criteria used to evaluate preferred shares, providing practical insights and examples relevant to the Canadian financial landscape.

### Investment Quality Assessment

#### Preferred Dividend Coverage Ratio

The preferred dividend coverage ratio is a vital metric that measures a company's ability to pay dividends to preferred shareholders from its earnings. It is calculated as:

{{< katex >}} \text{Preferred Dividend Coverage Ratio} = \frac{\text{Net Income}}{\text{Preferred Dividends}} {{< /katex >}}

A higher ratio indicates a stronger ability to meet dividend obligations, suggesting financial stability and lower risk for investors. For example, if a Canadian bank like RBC reports a net income of $10 billion and preferred dividends of $500 million, the coverage ratio would be:

{{< katex >}} \frac{10,000,000,000}{500,000,000} = 20 {{< /katex >}}

This ratio of 20 implies that RBC earns 20 times the amount needed to cover its preferred dividends, indicating a robust financial position.

#### Equity Cushion

The equity cushion represents the amount of equity backing each preferred share, providing a buffer against financial distress. It is calculated by dividing the total equity by the number of preferred shares outstanding:

{{< katex >}} \text{Equity Cushion} = \frac{\text{Total Equity}}{\text{Preferred Shares Outstanding}} {{< /katex >}}

A substantial equity cushion suggests that the company has adequate financial resources to support its preferred shares. For instance, if TD Bank has total equity of $50 billion and 1 million preferred shares, the equity cushion would be:

{{< katex >}} \frac{50,000,000,000}{1,000,000} = 50,000 {{< /katex >}}

This indicates that each preferred share is backed by $50,000 of equity, providing a strong safety net for investors.

#### Dividend Record

A company's dividend record is a historical indicator of its commitment to paying dividends to preferred shareholders. Consistent and uninterrupted dividend payments reflect financial strength and reliability. Investors should examine the company's dividend history over several years to assess its stability. For example, a company like BCE Inc., with a 10-year history of uninterrupted preferred dividends, would be considered a reliable investment.

#### Credit Assessment

Credit ratings assigned by securities rating services such as DBRS Morningstar or Moody's provide an independent evaluation of a company's creditworthiness. Higher ratings indicate lower investment risk and greater confidence in the company's ability to meet its financial obligations. Investors should consider these ratings when assessing the quality of preferred shares. For example, a preferred share with an "A" rating is generally considered a safer investment than one with a "BBB" rating.

### Selecting Preferred Shares

When selecting preferred shares, investors should consider various features and compare them with similar investments to ensure competitive returns.

#### Features to Consider

- **Cumulative Dividends:** Ensure that any missed dividends are accumulated and paid out before common dividends.
- **Sinking Funds:** Check if the company has a sinking fund to retire preferred shares, which can enhance security.
- **Protective Provisions:** Look for provisions that protect preferred shareholders, such as voting rights in certain situations.

#### Comparing Dividend Yields

Investors should compare the dividend yields of preferred shares with other fixed-income investments to ensure they offer competitive returns. For example, if a preferred share offers a 5% yield, compare it with government bonds or corporate bonds of similar risk profiles.

#### Convertible Preferred Shares

Convertible preferred shares offer the option to convert into common stock, providing potential for capital appreciation. When evaluating these shares, consider:

- **Outlook for Common Stock:** Assess the potential growth of the underlying common stock.
- **Duration of Conversion Privilege:** Determine how long the conversion option is available and its impact on investment value.

For instance, if a preferred share of a Canadian tech company is convertible into common stock, and the company's stock is expected to rise significantly, the conversion feature could add substantial value.

### Practical Example: Analyzing a Preferred Share Portfolio

Consider a portfolio of preferred shares from major Canadian banks. To assess the investment quality:

1. **Calculate the Preferred Dividend Coverage Ratio** for each bank to ensure they can comfortably meet dividend obligations.
2. **Evaluate the Equity Cushion** to confirm adequate financial backing for each preferred share.
3. **Review the Dividend Record** to identify banks with a strong history of consistent payments.
4. **Check Credit Ratings** to gauge the risk level of each investment.

By systematically applying these criteria, investors can make informed decisions and optimize their preferred share portfolios for stability and income.

### Best Practices and Common Pitfalls

- **Best Practices:**
  - Regularly monitor financial statements and credit ratings.
  - Diversify preferred share holdings across different sectors.
  - Stay informed about market trends and economic conditions.

- **Common Pitfalls:**
  - Ignoring credit ratings and financial metrics.
  - Overlooking the impact of interest rate changes on preferred share prices.
  - Failing to consider the implications of callable features.

### Conclusion

Assessing the investment quality of preferred shares involves a comprehensive analysis of financial metrics, credit ratings, and specific share features. By understanding these criteria and applying them to real-world scenarios, investors can enhance their portfolios and achieve their financial goals. As you continue your journey in the Canadian securities market, remember to leverage these insights and strategies to make informed investment decisions.
