## 25.3.6 Documentation for Managed Accounts

Managed accounts, particularly fee-based ones, are a cornerstone of personalized investment services in Canada. They offer clients tailored investment strategies managed by professional portfolio managers. However, the establishment and maintenance of these accounts require meticulous documentation and adherence to regulatory standards. This section delves into the critical aspects of documentation for managed accounts, emphasizing regulatory requirements, client agreements, supervisory approvals, and ongoing compliance.

### Regulatory Requirements for Managed Accounts

In Canada, managed accounts are subject to stringent regulatory oversight to protect investors and ensure transparency. The Canadian Investment Regulatory Organization (CIRO) and provincial securities commissions set forth guidelines that financial institutions must follow when setting up and managing these accounts.

#### Key Regulatory Documentation Requirements

1. **Client Relationship Disclosure**: Firms must provide clients with clear and comprehensive information about the nature of the relationship, including the services offered, fees charged, and the responsibilities of both the client and the firm.

2. **Know Your Client (KYC) Documentation**: This is a fundamental requirement where firms must gather detailed information about the client's financial situation, investment knowledge, risk tolerance, and investment objectives. This documentation ensures that the investment strategy aligns with the client's needs.

3. **Investment Policy Statement (IPS)**: An IPS outlines the client's investment goals and the strategies to achieve them. It serves as a roadmap for the portfolio manager and must be reviewed and updated regularly.

4. **Client Agreement**: This legally binding document specifies the terms and conditions of the managed account, including fee structures, investment strategies, and the roles and responsibilities of both parties.

5. **Supervisory Approval**: Before a managed account is activated, it must receive approval from a supervisory authority within the firm. This ensures that all regulatory requirements are met and that the account setup aligns with the firm's policies.

### Establishing Managed Accounts

The process of establishing a managed account involves several steps, each requiring specific documentation and approvals.

#### Step-by-Step Process

1. **Initial Consultation and KYC Process**: The relationship begins with an in-depth consultation to understand the client's financial situation and investment goals. This involves completing KYC forms and gathering necessary documentation.

2. **Drafting the Investment Policy Statement**: Based on the KYC information, an IPS is drafted to outline the investment strategy. This document is crucial for aligning the portfolio manager's actions with the client's objectives.

3. **Client Agreement Execution**: The client agreement is prepared, detailing the terms of the managed account. This document must be reviewed and signed by the client, ensuring they understand the fees, risks, and investment approach.

4. **Supervisory Review and Approval**: The completed documentation is reviewed by a supervisory authority within the firm. This step is critical for ensuring compliance with regulatory standards and firm policies.

5. **Account Activation**: Once all documentation is in place and approvals are obtained, the managed account is activated, and the portfolio manager can begin implementing the investment strategy.

### Importance of Clear Documentation

Clear and comprehensive documentation is vital for several reasons:

- **Alignment with Client Objectives**: Proper documentation ensures that the investment strategy is tailored to the client's specific goals and risk tolerance. This alignment is crucial for achieving desired outcomes and maintaining client satisfaction.

- **Regulatory Compliance**: Adhering to documentation requirements helps firms comply with regulatory standards, reducing the risk of legal issues and penalties.

- **Transparency and Trust**: Detailed documentation fosters transparency, helping to build trust between the client and the firm. Clients are more likely to feel confident in their investment decisions when they understand the terms and strategies involved.

### Ongoing Account Reviews and Supervisory Oversight

Managed accounts require continuous monitoring to ensure they remain aligned with the client's evolving goals and market conditions. Regular reviews and supervisory oversight are essential components of this process.

#### Best Practices for Ongoing Management

1. **Periodic Account Reviews**: Regularly scheduled reviews of the account's performance and strategy are necessary to ensure alignment with the client's objectives. These reviews should consider changes in the client's financial situation or market conditions.

2. **Supervisory Oversight**: Continuous oversight by a supervisory authority ensures that the account management adheres to regulatory standards and firm policies. This oversight helps identify and address any discrepancies or issues promptly.

3. **Client Communication**: Maintaining open lines of communication with the client is crucial. Regular updates and discussions about the account's performance and any necessary adjustments help maintain transparency and trust.

### Conclusion

In summary, the documentation for managed accounts is a critical aspect of their successful establishment and management. By adhering to regulatory requirements, ensuring clear and comprehensive documentation, and maintaining ongoing oversight, financial institutions can effectively manage these accounts to meet client objectives and regulatory standards. As the financial landscape evolves, staying informed about regulatory changes and best practices is essential for continued success in managing fee-based accounts.
