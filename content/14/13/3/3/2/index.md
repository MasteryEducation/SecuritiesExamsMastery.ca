## 25.3.3.2 Multi-Mandate Managed Accounts (UMAs)

In the evolving landscape of wealth management, Multi-Mandate Managed Accounts, also known as Unified Managed Accounts (UMAs), have emerged as a sophisticated solution for investors seeking diversified and professionally managed portfolios. This section delves into the structure, benefits, and challenges of UMAs, with a particular focus on the Canadian financial context.

### Understanding Multi-Mandate Managed Accounts (UMAs)

Multi-Mandate Managed Accounts are investment accounts that integrate multiple investment strategies, each managed by different portfolio managers. This structure allows for a comprehensive approach to asset management, where various asset classes and investment styles are combined within a single account. The primary goal of UMAs is to provide investors with a diversified portfolio that is tailored to their specific financial goals and risk tolerance.

#### The Role of Overlay Managers

A critical component of UMAs is the overlay manager. Overlay managers are responsible for coordinating the various investment strategies within the account. They ensure that the overall portfolio aligns with the investor's objectives and risk profile. Overlay managers perform several key functions:

- **Asset Allocation:** They determine the optimal allocation of assets across different mandates to achieve the desired balance between risk and return.
- **Risk Management:** Overlay managers monitor the portfolio's risk exposure and make adjustments as necessary to maintain the desired risk level.
- **Tax Efficiency:** They implement strategies to minimize tax liabilities, such as tax-loss harvesting and strategic asset location.
- **Rebalancing:** Overlay managers regularly rebalance the portfolio to ensure it remains aligned with the investor's goals and market conditions.

### Benefits of Multi-Mandate Managed Accounts

UMAs offer several advantages that make them an attractive option for investors:

1. **Diversified Management:** By incorporating multiple investment strategies, UMAs provide diversification across asset classes, sectors, and geographic regions. This diversification can help reduce portfolio volatility and enhance returns.

2. **Comprehensive Oversight:** The presence of an overlay manager ensures that the portfolio is managed holistically, with a focus on achieving the investor's overall financial objectives.

3. **Customization:** UMAs can be tailored to meet the specific needs and preferences of individual investors, allowing for personalized investment solutions.

4. **Efficiency:** By consolidating multiple strategies into a single account, UMAs simplify the investment process and reduce administrative burdens for investors.

### Challenges of Multi-Mandate Managed Accounts

Despite their benefits, UMAs also present certain challenges:

1. **Complexity:** Managing multiple mandates within a single account can be complex, requiring sophisticated systems and expertise to ensure effective coordination and oversight.

2. **Higher Fees:** The involvement of multiple portfolio managers and an overlay manager can lead to higher management fees compared to traditional investment accounts.

3. **Potential for Overlap:** Without careful management, there is a risk of overlap between the strategies of different managers, which can lead to inefficiencies and reduced diversification.

### Practical Example: Canadian Pension Funds

Canadian pension funds often utilize multi-mandate structures to manage their large and diverse portfolios. For instance, a pension fund might employ different managers for equities, fixed income, and alternative investments, with an overlay manager ensuring that the overall portfolio remains aligned with the fund's long-term objectives. This approach allows pension funds to leverage specialized expertise while maintaining a cohesive investment strategy.

### Case Study: RBC Wealth Management

RBC Wealth Management offers UMAs as part of its suite of investment solutions. By integrating multiple investment strategies and employing overlay management, RBC provides clients with a comprehensive and customized approach to wealth management. This structure allows RBC to deliver tailored investment solutions that align with clients' unique financial goals and risk profiles.

### Best Practices for Managing UMAs

To effectively manage UMAs, consider the following best practices:

- **Select Experienced Managers:** Choose portfolio managers with a proven track record in their respective asset classes and investment styles.
- **Implement Robust Systems:** Utilize advanced technology and systems to facilitate seamless coordination and oversight of multiple mandates.
- **Regularly Review and Adjust:** Continuously monitor the portfolio's performance and make adjustments as needed to ensure alignment with the investor's objectives.
- **Communicate Clearly:** Maintain open and transparent communication with clients to ensure they understand the structure and benefits of their UMA.

### Conclusion

Multi-Mandate Managed Accounts offer a powerful solution for investors seeking diversified and professionally managed portfolios. By integrating multiple investment strategies and employing overlay management, UMAs provide comprehensive oversight and customization. However, they also present challenges that require careful management and expertise. By understanding the intricacies of UMAs, investors can leverage their benefits while mitigating potential risks.
