## 25.3.3 Separately Managed Accounts (SMAs)

Separately Managed Accounts (SMAs) are a sophisticated investment vehicle that offers investors the opportunity to own individual securities directly, with the added benefit of a personalized investment strategy. Unlike mutual funds, where investors own shares of a pooled investment, SMAs provide direct ownership of the securities within the account, allowing for greater customization and control over investment decisions.

### Understanding Separately Managed Accounts (SMAs)

At their core, SMAs are investment accounts managed by professional portfolio managers. Each account is tailored to meet the specific financial goals and risk tolerance of the individual investor. This customization is achieved through a personalized investment strategy, which can include a wide range of asset classes such as equities, fixed income, and alternative investments.

#### Key Features of SMAs

- **Direct Ownership:** Investors in SMAs own the individual securities within their account, providing transparency and control over their investments.
- **Customization:** Investment strategies are tailored to the specific needs and objectives of the investor, allowing for personalized asset allocation and security selection.
- **Professional Management:** SMAs are managed by experienced portfolio managers who make investment decisions based on the investor's goals and market conditions.

### Single-Mandate vs. Multi-Mandate SMAs

SMAs can be categorized into two main types: single-mandate and multi-mandate accounts. Understanding the differences between these two structures is crucial for investors seeking to optimize their investment strategy.

#### Single-Mandate SMAs

Single-mandate SMAs focus on a specific investment strategy or asset class. For example, a single-mandate SMA might concentrate on Canadian equities, providing investors with exposure to a particular market segment. This approach allows for specialized management and can be beneficial for investors looking to capitalize on specific market opportunities.

#### Multi-Mandate SMAs

In contrast, multi-mandate SMAs offer a diversified approach by incorporating multiple investment strategies or asset classes within a single account. This structure provides a comprehensive investment solution, balancing risk and return across various market segments. Multi-mandate SMAs are ideal for investors seeking a holistic investment strategy that aligns with their overall financial goals.

### Advantages and Challenges of SMAs

While SMAs offer numerous benefits, they also present certain challenges that investors must consider.

#### Advantages of SMAs

- **Tailored Investment Strategies:** SMAs provide the flexibility to customize investment strategies based on individual preferences, risk tolerance, and financial objectives.
- **Tax Efficiency:** Direct ownership of securities allows for tax-loss harvesting and other tax-efficient strategies, potentially enhancing after-tax returns.
- **Transparency and Control:** Investors have full visibility into the securities held within their account, enabling informed decision-making and greater control over their investments.

#### Challenges of SMAs

- **Higher Fees:** SMAs typically involve higher management fees compared to pooled investment vehicles like mutual funds, due to the personalized nature of the service.
- **Complexity:** The customization and direct ownership aspects of SMAs can add complexity to the investment process, requiring a higher level of engagement and understanding from the investor.

### Aligning SMAs with Financial Goals

SMAs are particularly well-suited for investors with specific financial goals that require a tailored approach. Whether it's achieving a certain level of income, preserving capital, or growing wealth, SMAs offer the flexibility to design an investment strategy that aligns with these objectives.

#### Case Study: Canadian Pension Fund

Consider a Canadian pension fund seeking to optimize its asset allocation to meet long-term liabilities. By utilizing a multi-mandate SMA, the fund can diversify its investments across Canadian equities, fixed income, and alternative assets, ensuring a balanced approach that aligns with its risk tolerance and return objectives.

### Practical Application and Best Practices

For investors considering SMAs, it's essential to engage with a qualified portfolio manager who can provide expert guidance and support. Here are some best practices to consider:

- **Define Clear Objectives:** Clearly articulate your financial goals and risk tolerance to ensure the SMA strategy aligns with your needs.
- **Regularly Review Performance:** Monitor the performance of your SMA and make adjustments as needed to stay on track with your objectives.
- **Stay Informed:** Keep abreast of market trends and developments that may impact your investment strategy.

### Conclusion

Separately Managed Accounts offer a unique opportunity for investors to achieve personalized investment solutions through direct ownership and tailored strategies. While they come with certain challenges, the benefits of customization, tax efficiency, and transparency make SMAs a compelling choice for many investors. By understanding the intricacies of SMAs and aligning them with your financial goals, you can harness their potential to enhance your investment portfolio.
