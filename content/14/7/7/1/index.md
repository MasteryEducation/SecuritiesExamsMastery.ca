## 19.7.1 Core and Satellite Strategy

The Core and Satellite Strategy is a sophisticated investment approach that combines the stability of broad market exposure with the targeted growth potential of specialized investments. This strategy is particularly effective when utilizing Exchange-Traded Funds (ETFs), offering investors a balanced pathway to achieving their financial goals. In this section, we will delve into the intricacies of this strategy, focusing on the identification of core and satellite holdings, portfolio construction, and the benefits of this approach.

### Core Holdings Identification

Core holdings form the foundation of a diversified investment portfolio. These are typically broad market ETFs that provide stable returns and are characterized by:

- **Broad Market Exposure:** Core ETFs often track major indices such as the S&P/TSX Composite Index, offering exposure to a wide range of sectors and industries.
- **Low Management Expense Ratios (MERs):** Core ETFs generally have lower fees, making them cost-effective for long-term investment.
- **High Liquidity:** These ETFs are highly liquid, ensuring ease of buying and selling without significant price impact.

#### Example of Core Holdings

Consider the iShares Core S&P/TSX Capped Composite Index ETF (XIC), which provides exposure to the Canadian equity market. Its low MER and broad market coverage make it an ideal core holding for Canadian investors seeking stability.

### Satellite Holdings Selection

Satellite holdings are specialized ETFs that target specific sectors, regions, or investment themes. These holdings aim to enhance returns and add diversification to the portfolio. Key characteristics include:

- **Targeted Exposure:** Satellite ETFs focus on niche markets or sectors, such as technology, healthcare, or emerging markets.
- **Higher Growth Potential:** These ETFs are selected for their potential to outperform the broader market.
- **Increased Volatility:** Due to their focused nature, satellite ETFs may exhibit higher volatility compared to core holdings.

#### Example of Satellite Holdings

An example of a satellite ETF is the BMO MSCI Tech & Industrial Innovation Index ETF (ZIN), which targets the technology and industrial innovation sectors. This ETF can complement core holdings by capturing growth opportunities in high-potential industries.

### Portfolio Construction

Constructing a portfolio using the Core and Satellite Strategy involves a strategic allocation of investments between core and satellite ETFs. The goal is to balance stability with growth potential:

1. **Determine Core Allocation:** Typically, the core portion constitutes 60-80% of the portfolio, providing a stable base.
2. **Select Satellite ETFs:** Allocate 20-40% to satellite ETFs, focusing on areas with higher growth potential or specific investment themes.
3. **Regular Rebalancing:** Periodically review and adjust the portfolio to maintain the desired allocation and respond to market changes.

#### Portfolio Example

Consider a portfolio with 70% allocated to core ETFs like XIC and 30% to satellite ETFs such as ZIN. This allocation provides a solid foundation while allowing for targeted growth through specialized sectors.

### Implementation Examples

Let's examine how varying allocations between core and satellite holdings can impact portfolio performance:

- **Conservative Approach:** A portfolio with 80% core and 20% satellite ETFs focuses on stability, suitable for risk-averse investors.
- **Aggressive Approach:** A 60% core and 40% satellite allocation targets higher growth, appealing to investors with a higher risk tolerance.

### Benefits of Core and Satellite Strategy

The Core and Satellite Strategy offers several advantages:

- **Diversification:** By combining broad market exposure with targeted investments, this strategy reduces risk while capturing growth opportunities.
- **Cost Efficiency:** Utilizing low-cost core ETFs minimizes expenses, enhancing overall returns.
- **Flexibility:** Investors can adjust satellite holdings to capitalize on emerging trends or market shifts.

### Glossary

- **Core Holdings:** Broad market ETFs that form the foundation of a diversified investment portfolio.
- **Satellite Holdings:** Specialized or sector-specific ETFs used to enhance portfolio returns or target specific investment themes.
- **Portfolio Construction:** The process of selecting and allocating investments to build a diversified and balanced portfolio.
- **Diversification:** The practice of spreading investments across various assets to reduce risk.

### Conclusion

The Core and Satellite Strategy is a powerful tool for constructing a well-balanced ETF portfolio. By carefully selecting core and satellite holdings, investors can achieve a blend of stability and growth tailored to their financial objectives. This approach not only enhances diversification but also provides the flexibility to adapt to changing market conditions.
