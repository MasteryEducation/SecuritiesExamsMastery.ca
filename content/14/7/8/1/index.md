## 19.8.1 Mutual Funds of ETFs

Mutual funds of ETFs represent an innovative approach to investment management, combining the benefits of mutual funds and exchange-traded funds (ETFs) to offer investors a diversified, cost-effective, and flexible investment vehicle. This section delves into the structure, benefits, and strategic applications of mutual funds of ETFs, with a focus on the Canadian financial context.

### Structure and Composition

Mutual funds of ETFs are structured to hold a diversified portfolio of various ETFs rather than individual securities. This unique composition allows investors to gain exposure to a broad range of asset classes, sectors, and geographic regions through a single investment vehicle. By investing in a mutual fund of ETFs, investors benefit from the inherent diversification of ETFs, which typically track indices or specific market segments.

**Benefits of Structure:**
- **Enhanced Diversification:** By holding multiple ETFs, these mutual funds provide exposure to a wide array of assets, reducing the risk associated with individual securities.
- **Simplified Portfolio Management:** Investors can achieve broad market exposure and diversification without the need to manage multiple individual ETF holdings.

### Automatic Rebalancing

One of the key advantages of mutual funds of ETFs is the feature of automatic rebalancing. This process involves realigning the portfolio's asset allocation to maintain the desired investment strategy, typically based on predefined rules or thresholds.

**Cost Efficiencies:**
- **Reduced Trading Costs:** Automatic rebalancing minimizes the need for frequent manual trades, thereby reducing transaction costs.
- **Maintained Asset Allocation:** Ensures that the portfolio remains aligned with the investor's risk tolerance and investment objectives without incurring significant trading costs.

### Access to PACs and SWPs

Mutual funds of ETFs offer investors the convenience of Pre-Authorized Contributions (PACs) and Systematic Withdrawal Plans (SWPs), providing flexibility in investment and withdrawal processes.

**PACs (Pre-Authorized Contributions):**
- Allow investors to make regular, automatic contributions to their mutual fund accounts, facilitating disciplined saving and investment habits.

**SWPs (Systematic Withdrawal Plans):**
- Enable investors to set up regular, automatic withdrawals from their mutual fund accounts, providing a steady income stream or liquidity as needed.

### Cost Comparison

A significant advantage of mutual funds of ETFs is their typically lower Management Expense Ratios (MERs) compared to traditional mutual funds. This cost efficiency is achieved through the aggregation of ETFs, which generally have lower fees than actively managed mutual funds.

**Impact on Net Returns:**
- **Lower MERs:** Contribute to improved net returns for investors by reducing the overall cost of investment management.
- **Enhanced Performance:** Over time, lower fees can significantly enhance the compounding effect on investment returns.

### Investment Strategies within Mutual Funds of ETFs

Mutual funds of ETFs can be structured to implement various investment strategies, ranging from static to tactical approaches.

**Static Strategies:**
- Focus on maintaining a consistent asset allocation aligned with long-term investment objectives.

**Tactical Strategies:**
- Involve active adjustments to ETF holdings based on market conditions, economic forecasts, or specific investment opportunities.

**Role of Portfolio Managers:**
- Portfolio managers play a crucial role in selecting and adjusting ETF holdings to align with the fund's investment objectives and market outlook.

### Examples of Mutual Funds of ETFs

To illustrate the practical application of mutual funds of ETFs, let's review some examples of these funds, focusing on their investment objectives and performance metrics.

**Example 1: Canadian Balanced ETF Fund**
- **Objective:** Achieve a balanced mix of equity and fixed-income exposure through a diversified portfolio of Canadian and international ETFs.
- **Performance Metrics:** Evaluated based on risk-adjusted returns, volatility, and alignment with benchmark indices.

**Example 2: Global Equity Growth ETF Fund**
- **Objective:** Provide long-term capital appreciation by investing in a diversified portfolio of global equity ETFs.
- **Performance Metrics:** Focus on growth potential, sector diversification, and geographic exposure.

### Glossary

- **Mutual Funds of ETFs:** Mutual funds that invest in a diversified portfolio of ETFs instead of individual securities.
- **PACs (Pre-Authorized Contributions):** Regular, automatic contributions made to a mutual fund account.
- **SWPs (Systematic Withdrawal Plans):** Regular, automatic withdrawals made from a mutual fund account.
- **Automatic Rebalancing:** The process of realigning a portfolio's asset allocation automatically based on predefined rules or thresholds.
- **Management Expense Ratio (MER):** The total annual fees expressed as a percentage of the fund’s average net assets, covering management and operational costs.

### Conclusion

Mutual funds of ETFs offer a compelling investment option for Canadian investors seeking diversification, cost efficiency, and flexibility. By understanding the structure, benefits, and strategic applications of these funds, investors can make informed decisions that align with their financial goals. As the investment landscape continues to evolve, mutual funds of ETFs represent a dynamic and adaptable solution for modern portfolio management.
